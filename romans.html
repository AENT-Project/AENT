Romans

The Book of Romans — (AENT) Accessible English New Testament

This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.

A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."

This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.

Grace & Peace.

Romans Chapter 1

Paul Introduces Himself and His Message

I am Paul, a servant of Jesus the Messiah. God chose me to be an apostle and set me apart to tell his good news.

God promised this good news a long time ago through his prophets, and it's written in the Holy Scriptures. This news is about his Son — who as a human being was born into the family of King David, but through the Holy Spirit was shown to be the powerful Son of God by being raised from the dead. He is Jesus the Messiah, our Lord.

Through him, God gave me the privilege of being an apostle. My work is to call people from every nation to trust and obey him — all for the honor of his name. You in Rome are among those who have been called to belong to Jesus the Messiah.

Greeting to the Church in Rome

I'm writing to all of you in Rome. God loves you and has called you to be his holy people. May you have grace and peace from God our Father and from the Lord Jesus the Messiah.

Paul's Desire to Visit Rome

First, I thank my God through Jesus the Messiah for all of you, because people all over the world are talking about your faith. God is my witness — I serve him with all my heart by telling the good news about his Son — and I always remember you in my prayers. I pray that if it's God's will, I'll finally be able to come and see you.

I want very much to see you so I can share something with you that will help you stand strong in your faith. And when I come, we'll be able to encourage each other.

I want you to know, brothers and sisters, that I've planned to visit you many times, but something has always stopped me. I want to see good results among you, just as I've seen among other non-Jewish peoples. I have a duty to all people — those who are educated and those who are not, those who are wise and those who are not. That's why I'm so eager to tell the good news to you in Rome.

The Power of the Good News

I'm not ashamed of the good news. It is the power of God to save everyone who trusts him — first for the Jews, and now for everyone else too. This good news shows us how God makes us right with himself, from trusting him at the start all the way to the end. As the Scripture says, "The person who is right with God will live by trusting him."

The Consequences of Rejecting God

But God shows his anger from heaven against all the sin and evil of people who hide the truth by the wrong things they do. What can be known about God is plain to them because God has made it plain. Ever since the world was created, people have been able to see his invisible qualities — his eternal power and his divine nature — in everything he has made. So people have no excuse.

They knew about God, but they didn't honor him or thank him. Instead their thinking became worthless and their confused hearts grew dark. They claimed to be wise, but they became fools. They traded the glory of the immortal God for statues made to look like humans, birds, animals, and snakes.

So God let them go their own way and follow the sinful desires of their hearts. They did shameful things with each other's bodies. They traded the truth of God for a lie and worshiped and served created things instead of the One who created them — the One who should be praised forever. Amen.

Because of this, God let them follow their shameful passions. Even the women traded natural relationships for ones that go against nature. In the same way, the men stopped having natural relationships with women and burned with desire for each other. Men did shameful things with other men, and because of this they suffered in their own bodies the consequences they deserved.

Since they didn't think it was important to know God, he let them have useless minds that lead them to do things that should never be done. Their lives are full of every kind of sin, evil, greed, and hate. They are full of jealousy, murder, fighting, lying, and thinking the worst of others. They gossip and speak evil of each other. They hate God. They are rude, proud, and brag about themselves. They find new ways to do evil. They don't obey their parents. They have no sense, they break their promises, and they show no kindness or mercy.

They know that God's law says people who live like this deserve to die. Yet they not only keep doing these things — they cheer for others who do them too.

Romans Chapter 2

You Have No Excuse

You may think you have an excuse, but you don't. If you judge others, you are condemning yourself — because you do the very same things you judge others for doing. We know that God is right when he judges people who do these things. You judge others for their sins, but then you do those same things. Do you really think you'll escape the judgment of God?

Or do you think his great kindness and patience mean nothing? Don't you see that God is being patient with you so that you'll change your heart and turn to him?

But you are stubborn. You refuse to change. You are piling up anger against yourself for the day when God shows his anger. On that day, everyone will see that his judgment is fair. He will give to each person exactly what they deserve for what they have done. He will give eternal life to those who keep doing good and who seek his glory and honor. But there will be anger and fury for those who are selfish — those who refuse to follow the truth and choose to do evil instead.

There will be trouble and suffering for every person who does evil — for the Jew first, and also for the non-Jew. But God will give glory, honor, and peace to everyone who does good — for the Jew first, and also for the non-Jew. God doesn't play favorites.

God Judges Everyone Fairly

People who sin without ever hearing God's written law will still be lost. And people who sin while having the law will be judged by that law. Just hearing the law doesn't make a person right with God — only those who obey it will be made right.

Sometimes people who are not Jews do the things the law requires, even though they've never had the written law. They show that they know in their hearts what is right and wrong. Their own thoughts and conscience tell them when they're doing wrong or when they're doing right. This will all come out on the day God judges the secret thoughts of everyone through Jesus the Messiah, just as the good news says.

The Jews and the Law

Now, what about you? You call yourself a Jew. You trust in the law and brag about your relationship with God. You know what he wants. You've been taught the law, so you know the difference between right and wrong. You believe you are a guide for the blind and a light for people in the dark. You think you can teach foolish people and instruct children. You believe you have all knowledge and truth because you have the law.

But you teach others — why don't you teach yourself? You tell others not to steal, but do you steal? You say people should not commit adultery, but do you? You hate statues of false gods, but do you steal from their temples? You brag about having the law, but you bring shame to God by breaking it. As the Scriptures say, "The other nations speak evil of God's name because of you."

What Really Makes Someone a Jew

Being a Jew has value if you obey the law. But if you break the law, it's as if you were never a Jew at all. If a man who is not a Jew obeys the law, won't God accept him as if he were a Jew? In fact, the person who obeys the law without the physical mark of a Jew will stand in judgment over you — you who have the written law and the physical mark but still break the law.

A person is not a Jew just because of how they look on the outside. True membership isn't something done only to the body. A real Jew is one on the inside. True change is something that happens in the heart — a work of the Spirit, not just following a written rule. A person like that doesn't look for praise from people, but from God.

Romans Chapter 3

The Advantage of Being a Jew

So is there any advantage to being a Jew? Is there value in being one of God's chosen people? Yes — a great deal of value in every way. Most importantly, God trusted the Jews to keep his holy words.

What if some of them weren't faithful? Does their failure make God unfaithful? Not at all. God will always be true, even if every human being is a liar. As the Scripture says, "Your words will be proven right, and you will win when you are judged."

But someone might say, "If our wrongdoing makes God's righteousness shine even brighter, is it fair for him to punish us?" Of course it is. If God weren't fair, how could he judge the world? Others might ask, "If my lie brings more glory to God by showing how truthful he is, why am I still judged as a sinner?" Some people even twist our words and claim we say, "Let's do evil so that good will come from it." Anyone who says that deserves exactly what they get.

Everyone Is Under the Power of Sin

So what's the conclusion? Are Jews better than everyone else? No. I've already shown that everyone — Jews and non-Jews alike — is under the power of sin. As the Scriptures say:

"No one is right with God. Not even one. No one understands. No one is truly looking for God. Everyone has turned away. Everyone has become worthless together. No one does what is good — not even one person."

"Their throats are like open graves. They use their tongues to tell lies. The words from their lips are like snake poison. Their mouths are full of cursing and bitterness. They are quick to hurt and kill. They leave behind destruction and misery. They don't know the way of peace. They have no fear of God."

Everything the law says is spoken to those who live under the law. It stops everyone from making excuses and holds the whole world accountable to God. No one will be made right with God by trying to obey the law. The law only exists to show us clearly what sin is.

Made Right Through Faith

But now God has shown us a different way to be made right with him — and it has nothing to do with keeping the law. The law and the prophets pointed to this all along. God makes people right with himself through faith in Jesus the Messiah. This is for everyone who believes — no exceptions. Every single person has sinned. Every single person has fallen short of what God intended us to be.

But God in his kindness gives us a free gift. He makes us right with himself at no cost to us. He did this by paying the price to set us free — through Jesus the Messiah. God offered Jesus as the way to take away our sins. Because of his death, we are forgiven when we trust in him.

God did this to show that he is fair. In the past he was patient and held back punishment for sins. But now he has shown once and for all that he is completely right and fair — and that he makes right anyone who puts their trust in Jesus.

Faith, Not the Law

So do we have anything to brag about? No. Why not? Because we didn't earn this by obeying the law. It came through faith. We believe that a person is made right with God by trusting him — not by doing what the law requires.

Is God only the God of the Jews? Isn't he the God of non-Jews too? Yes, he is — there is only one God. He makes the Jews right through faith, and he makes the non-Jews right through that same faith.

Does this mean we're throwing out the law because of faith? Not at all. We're actually showing what the law was always meant to do.

Romans Chapter 4

Abraham Was Made Right by Faith

So what can we say about Abraham, the father of our people? What did he discover about all this? If Abraham was made right with God because of the good things he did, he would have something to brag about. But not before God.

What do the Scriptures say? They say, "Abraham trusted God, and God accepted that faith as making him right."

When a person works, their pay isn't a gift — it's something they earned. But if a person stops trying to earn it and simply trusts the One who makes even sinful people right with God, that faith is what saves them.

David Says the Same Thing

David says the same thing. He tells us how happy a person is when God accepts them as right without looking at what they've done. David said:

"How happy are those whose wrongs are forgiven. How happy are those whose sins are covered up. How happy is the person whose sin the Lord will never count against them."

Was Abraham Marked Before or After?

Is this happiness only for Jews? Or is it for everyone? We've already said that Abraham's faith was accepted as making him right with God. But when did this happen — before or after he received the mark of our people?

It was before. He wasn't yet marked when God accepted him. He received the mark later as a sign — a seal to show that he was already right with God because of the faith he had before the mark ever came.

So Abraham is the father of all who trust God, marked or not. God accepts them all as right. And he is the father of those who are marked — but only of those who do more than follow a rule. He is the father of those who walk the same path of faith that Abraham walked before he was ever marked.

The Promise Comes Through Faith, Not the Law

God promised Abraham and his children that they would inherit the world. This promise didn't come because Abraham obeyed the law — it came because he was made right with God through faith. If the promise only went to those who keep the law, then faith would mean nothing and the promise would be worthless.

The law brings anger because we can't keep it. But where there is no law, there is no breaking of the law. That's why the promise depends on faith — it's a free gift. And because it's a gift, the promise is certain for all of Abraham's children. Not just for those who have the law, but for everyone who has the same faith as Abraham. He is the father of us all. As the Scripture says, "I have made you the father of many nations."

Abraham's Unshakeable Faith

Abraham believed in the God who gives life to the dead — the God who speaks of things that don't yet exist as if they're already here. There was no human reason to hope, but Abraham kept hoping and trusting anyway. Because he did, he became the father of many nations, just as God had promised: "You will have many children."

Abraham was about a hundred years old. He knew his body was as good as dead and that Sarah couldn't have children. But his faith didn't get weak. He didn't doubt God's promise. He didn't lose faith. Instead, his faith made him strong, and he gave glory to God. He was completely sure that God had the power to do what he had promised. This is why "God accepted his faith as making him right."

Written for Us Too

These words weren't written only for Abraham. They were written for us too. God will accept us as right if we trust in him — the One who raised Jesus our Lord from the dead. Jesus was handed over to die because of the wrong things we did, and he was raised to life so that we could be made right with God.

Romans Chapter 5

Peace with God

Since we've been made right with God through faith, we have peace with him through our Lord Jesus the Messiah. Through him we've been given access by faith into this grace that we now stand in. And we don't just get by — we celebrate in the hope of sharing God's glory.

But that's not all. We even celebrate in our hardships. Why? Because hardship builds grit, and grit builds character, and character builds hope. And this hope never lets us down, because God has flooded our hearts with his love through the Holy Spirit he gave us.

Christ Died for Us While We Were Still Sinners

Think about it — while we were still completely helpless, at just the right moment, Christ died for people who didn't care about God. You'll rarely find someone willing to die even for a good person — though for a truly exceptional person, someone might be brave enough to try. But God proves his love for us in this: while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.

So since we've been made right by his blood, it's even more certain that he will save us from God's judgment. If God reconciled us to himself through the death of his Son while we were still his enemies, it's even more certain that now — as his friends — we will be saved by his life. And not only that, but we actually celebrate in God through our Lord Jesus the Messiah, through whom we have now received this new relationship with God.

Adam and Christ

Here is how it works: One man — Adam — brought sin into the world. And sin brought death. Death then spread to every single person who ever lived, because every single person has sinned. Sin was already in the world before God gave Moses the written law, but without a specific rule to break, sin wasn't being counted the same way. Even so, death ruled over everyone from Adam all the way to Moses — even over people who hadn't broken a direct command the way Adam did. In that way, Adam was a preview of Jesus — the one who was still to come.

But here's the difference — Adam's failure and God's gift are not the same kind of thing at all. One man failed and many people died because of it. But God's grace is so much bigger than that. Through one man, Jesus the Messiah, grace overflowed and covered far more than one failure ever could.

And here's another difference. Adam made one mistake and the verdict came back guilty. But God's gift works the other way — it came after countless mistakes and brings back a verdict of not guilty. Death got its power through one man's one failure. But grace is far more powerful than death. Those who receive God's grace and are made right with him will actually reign in life — and all of this comes through one man, Jesus the Messiah.

Grace Is Greater Than Sin

So then, just as one failure brought a guilty verdict for everyone, one act of righteousness brings a not guilty verdict that gives life to everyone. Just as many were made sinners through one man's disobedience, many will be made right through one man's obedience.

The law came into the picture so that sin would be clearly seen for what it is. But where sin increased, grace overflowed even more. Just as sin once reigned through death, grace now reigns through righteousness — bringing eternal life through Jesus the Messiah our Lord.

Romans Chapter 6

Dead to Sin, Alive in Christ

So what should we conclude? Should we keep on sinning so that grace can keep on increasing? Absolutely not! We died to sin — how can we possibly keep living in it? Or have you forgotten that when we were baptized into Christ Jesus, we were baptized into his death?

We were buried with him through baptism into that death, so that just as Christ was raised from the dead by the Father's glory, we too can walk in a brand new kind of life. If we have been joined with him in a death like his, we will certainly be joined with him in a resurrection like his too.

Here's what we know: our old self was crucified with him so that sin's power over us would be broken. The goal is that we would no longer be slaves to sin. Think about it — a dead person is free from sin's claim. Now if we died with Christ, we believe we will also live with him. We know that since Christ was raised from the dead, he can never die again — death no longer has any power over him. The death he died, he died to sin once and for all. But the life he lives, he lives for God.

In the same way, think of yourselves as dead to sin but alive to God in Christ Jesus.

No Longer Slaves to Sin

So don't let sin run your life, making you give in to its cravings. Don't hand over any part of yourself to sin as a tool for doing wrong. Instead, offer yourselves to God as people who have been brought from death to life. Give every part of yourself to God as a tool for doing what is right. Sin is no longer your master — because you're not living under the law anymore. You're living under grace.

So should we sin because we're under grace and not the law? Absolutely not! Don't you realize that when you give yourself to someone as a slave, you belong to whoever you obey? You're either a slave to sin — which leads to death — or a slave to obedience — which leads to being right with God.

Slaves to God

But thank God! You used to be slaves to sin, but you chose from the heart to follow the teaching you were given. You were set free from sin and became devoted to doing what is right. I'm using the word slave here because it's the clearest way to say it. Just as you once gave every part of yourself over to impurity and lawlessness — which only led to more lawlessness — now give every part of yourself over to doing right, which leads to a holy life.

When you were slaves to sin, doing right had no hold on you at all. And what did that get you? Nothing but shame — and those things end in death. But now you've been set free from sin and belong to God. The life you're growing now leads to holiness, and where that road ends is eternal life.

Sin pays its workers with death. But God's gift is eternal life through Christ Jesus our Lord.

Romans Chapter 7

The Analogy of Marriage

Do you know, brothers and sisters — and I'm speaking to those who know the law — that the law only has authority over a person while they're alive? For example, a married woman is bound by law to her husband as long as he lives. But if her husband dies, she is free from that law. So if she gets together with another man while her husband is still alive, she is called an adulteress. But if her husband dies, she is free — and if she marries another man, she has done nothing wrong.

In the same way, my brothers and sisters, you died to the law through the body of Christ. Now you belong to another — to the one who was raised from the dead — so that you can live and grow for God. Before, when we were living according to our sinful nature, the law actually stirred up sinful passions inside us, and everything we produced led to death. But now we've died to what once held us prisoner. We've been set free from the law so that we can serve God in the new way of the Spirit, not in the old way of following a written code.

The Law and Sin

So what are we saying — is the law sinful? Of course not! But the law is what showed me what sin actually was. I would never have known what it meant to covet if the law hadn't said, "Do not covet." But sin grabbed hold of that command and used it to produce every kind of coveting inside me. Apart from the law, sin was just lying there, dead. I was alive before the law came. But when the command arrived, sin came to life — and I died. The very command that was meant to bring life actually brought death. Sin used the command to trick me and kill me.

So the law itself is holy. The command is holy, right, and good. Did something good become death to me? No. Sin used something good to bring about my death — so that sin could be seen for exactly what it is.

The Internal Conflict

I know that God's law is good and right. But I'm not. It's like I'm a slave to sin and can't break free. I don't even understand my own behavior sometimes. I want to do what is right, but I don't do it. I don't want to do what is wrong, but I do it anyway. And when I do what I don't want to do, I'm actually agreeing that the law is right — because I know better.

So what's going on? It's not really the best part of me doing these things — it's the sin that lives inside me. I know that nothing good lives in my sinful nature. I want to do good — I genuinely want to — but I can't seem to follow through. The good I want to do, I don't do. The wrong I don't want to do, I keep doing anyway. When that happens, it isn't really the true me acting — it's the sin living inside me that takes over.

So here's what I've discovered: even when I want to do good, evil is right there waiting. Deep inside I love God's law. But there's another force at work in me, fighting against what my mind knows is right. It takes me prisoner and hands me over to the sin that lives in me.

The Resolution

What a miserable condition I'm in! Who will rescue me from this life that is slowly dying? Thank God — he does it through Jesus the Messiah our Lord!

So then, in my mind I want to serve God's law — but my sinful nature makes me a slave to the law of sin.

Romans Chapter 8

No More Condemnation

So now there is no condemnation for those who belong to Christ Jesus. Why? Because the power of the life-giving Spirit in Christ Jesus has set you free from the power of sin and death. The law couldn't do this — our human nature made it too weak. But God did it. He sent his own Son in a human body like ours — a body capable of sin — and dealt with sin right there in human flesh. He did this so that everything the law required could be fully met in us — people who no longer live according to their old nature but according to the Spirit.

The Old Nature vs. The Spirit

People who live by their old nature have their minds fixed on what that nature wants. But those who live by the Spirit have their minds fixed on what the Spirit wants. When your old nature controls your mind, it leads to death. When the Spirit controls your mind, it leads to life and peace. The old nature is hostile toward God — it refuses to follow God's law and frankly isn't even capable of it. That's why people who are ruled by their old nature cannot please God.

But that's not you. You are controlled by the Spirit — if God's Spirit actually lives in you. And if anyone doesn't have the Spirit of Christ, they don't belong to him at all. If Christ is in you, your body is still heading toward death because of sin — but your spirit is alive because you've been made right with God. And if the Spirit of the One who raised Jesus from the dead lives in you, then God — the one who raised Christ — will also give life to your dying bodies through his Spirit living inside you.

God's Children and Heirs

So then brothers and sisters, we don't owe our old nature anything. We don't have to live the way it wants us to. If you live by that old nature, you're going to die. But if by the Spirit you put to death the wrong things your body keeps pushing you toward, you will live.

Anyone who is led by God's Spirit is a child of God. You didn't receive a spirit of slavery that makes you afraid. You received the Spirit that makes you God's own child. And by that Spirit we cry out, "Abba — Father!" The Spirit himself joins with our spirit to confirm that we are God's children. And if we are children, we are heirs — heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ. We share in his sufferings now so that we may share in his glory later.

Future Glory

What we're going through right now is nothing compared to the glory that God is going to reveal in us. In fact, all of creation is holding its breath, waiting for that day. Creation didn't choose to be broken — God allowed it to happen — but he did it with a promise attached. One day creation itself will be set free from death and decay and will share in the freedom and glory of God's children.

Think of it this way — all of creation has been groaning like a woman in labor, right up to this very moment. And it's not just creation. We groan too, even though we already have the Holy Spirit living in us. The Spirit is just the first taste of what's coming. We're still waiting for the rest — the day when God fully adopts us and rescues our bodies from death completely.

That's what we were saved for — a hope. And hope only makes sense for something you don't have yet. If you could already see it and touch it, you wouldn't need to hope for it. But when we're hoping for something we can't see yet, we wait for it with patience.

The Spirit Speaks for Us

In the same way, the Spirit helps us in our weakness. We don't even always know what we should pray for, but the Spirit himself speaks up for us with groans that go beyond words. And God, who searches our hearts, knows exactly what the Spirit is thinking — because the Spirit pleads for God's people in perfect harmony with God's own will.

We know that for those who love God, everything works together for good — for those who are called according to his purpose. Those God knew in advance, he chose in advance to be shaped into the image of his Son, so that the Son would be the firstborn among many brothers and sisters. And those he chose, he called. Those he called, he made right with himself. And those he made right, he brought into his glory.

More Than Conquerors

So what do we say about all this? If God is for us, who can possibly stand against us? He didn't even spare his own Son — he gave him up for all of us. Won't he also give us everything else along with him? Who will bring any charge against the people God has chosen? God is the one who makes us right — so who is left to condemn us? No one! Christ Jesus is the one who died — and even more than that, he was raised to life — and right now he is sitting at God's right hand, speaking up for us.

Who can separate us from the love of Christ? Can trouble? Hardship? Persecution? Hunger? Poverty? Danger? War? As the Scripture says, "For your sake we are being killed all day long — we are treated like sheep headed for slaughter."

No. In all these things we are more than conquerors through the one who loved us. I am absolutely convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, neither any power in heaven nor any power on earth, nor anything else in all creation will ever be able to separate us from the love of God that is found in Christ Jesus our Lord.

Romans Chapter 9

Paul's Deep Sorrow for His People

I am telling the truth as someone who belongs to Christ — I'm not lying. My conscience, guided by the Holy Spirit, confirms it: my heart is filled with a deep, never-ending sadness. I would honestly be willing to be cut off from Christ myself if it would help my own people — my Jewish brothers and sisters.

They are the people of Israel. God adopted them as his children. He showed them his glory. He made covenants with them. He gave them the law, the temple worship, and his promises. The great patriarchs belong to them, and Christ himself came from their family line — he who is God over all, praised forever. Amen.

God's Promise Has Not Failed

But this doesn't mean God's word has failed. Not everyone who descended from Israel is truly part of Israel. And just because people are descendants of Abraham doesn't make them all his true children. As the Scripture says, "Your descendants will be traced through Isaac." This means it isn't the naturally born children who are God's children — it's the children of the promise who count as Abraham's true offspring. God's promise was this: "At the right time I will return, and Sarah will have a son."

And there's more. Think about Rebekah's twin sons — same father, our ancestor Isaac. Before those twins were even born, before they had done anything good or bad, God made a choice. To show that his purpose depends on his own choice — not on what people do, but on his call — he told Rebekah, "The older son will serve the younger." As it is written: "I loved Jacob, but I rejected Esau."

Is God Being Unfair?

So what do we say? Is God being unfair? Absolutely not! He said to Moses, "I will show mercy to anyone I choose, and I will have compassion on anyone I choose." So it doesn't depend on what a person wants or how hard they work — it depends entirely on God's mercy. The Scripture says to Pharaoh: "I raised you up for this very reason — to demonstrate my power through you so that my name would be known all over the earth." So God shows mercy to those he wants to, and he hardens the hearts of those he wants to.

The Potter and the Clay

Now you might ask, "Then why does God still blame us? Who can resist what he wants to happen?" But who are you — a mere human — to argue with God? Does a pot say to the potter, "Why did you make me like this?" Doesn't the potter have the right to take the same lump of clay and make one jar for special occasions and another for everyday use?

What if God, wanting to show his anger and demonstrate his power, put up with people headed for destruction with great patience? And what if he did this to make the incredible riches of his glory known to those he prepared in advance for glory? That includes us — people he called not only from among the Jews but also from among the Gentiles.

The Gentiles Are Included

As God says in the book of Hosea:

"I will call those who were 'Not My People' My People, and I will call her who was 'Not Loved' My Loved One."

And, "In the very place where they were told, 'You are not my people,' they will be called children of the living God."

Isaiah also says this about Israel: "Even if the people of Israel are as many as the grains of sand on the seashore, only a remnant will be saved. For the Lord will carry out his sentence on the earth quickly and completely." And as Isaiah predicted: "If the Lord of Heaven's Armies hadn't left us some descendants, we would have ended up like Sodom and Gomorrah."

Israel Missed It

So what's the conclusion? The Gentiles, who weren't even looking for a right relationship with God, found one — a relationship based on faith. But Israel, who tried so hard to be right with God by following the law, never reached that goal. Why? Because they tried to get there through their own efforts instead of through faith. They tripped over the stumbling stone, just as it is written:

"Look — I am placing a stone in Jerusalem that makes people stumble, a rock that makes them fall. But whoever trusts in him will never be put to shame."

Romans Chapter 10

Paul's Prayer for Israel

Brothers and sisters, my heart's deepest desire and my prayer to God is that the people of Israel may be saved. I can say this about them — they are deeply passionate about God. But their passion isn't based on a true understanding of him. They didn't understand how God makes people right with himself, so they tried to set up their own way of doing it. In doing so, they refused to accept God's way. For Christ is the end of the law — he provides a right relationship with God for everyone who believes.

Faith vs. Following Rules

Moses wrote about the way of being right with God through keeping the law: "The person who does these things will find life through them." But the way of faith says something different: "Don't say in your heart, 'Who will go up to heaven?' — as if to bring Christ down — or 'Who will go down into the deep?' — as if to bring Christ up from the dead."

So what does it say? "The message is very close to you — it's in your mouth and in your heart." And that is exactly the message of faith we preach: If you openly declare with your mouth that Jesus is Lord, and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved. It's by believing in your heart that you are made right with God, and it's by openly declaring your faith that you are saved.

No Difference Between Anyone

As the Scripture says, "Anyone who trusts in him will never be put to shame." There is no difference between Jews and Gentiles in this — the same Lord is Lord over everyone, and he gives generously to everyone who calls on him. As the Scripture says, "Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved."

Someone Has to Tell Them

But how can people call on him if they haven't believed in him? And how can they believe in him if they've never heard about him? And how can they hear unless someone tells them? And how can anyone tell them unless they are sent? As the Scripture says, "How beautiful are the feet of those who bring good news!"

But not everyone accepted the good news. Isaiah said, "Lord, who has believed our message?" So faith comes from hearing, and hearing comes through the word about Christ.

Did Israel Hear? Did Israel Understand?

But I ask — did Israel not hear? Of course they did:

"The message has gone out to the whole earth — their words have reached the ends of the world."

Then did Israel not understand? First, Moses said:

"I will make you jealous of people who aren't even a nation. I will make you angry at a nation that has no understanding."

And Isaiah goes even further:

"I was found by people who weren't looking for me. I showed myself to those who weren't asking for me."

But about Israel, God says:

"All day long I have held out my hands to a disobedient and stubborn people."

Romans Chapter 11

God Has Not Rejected Israel

So I ask — did God reject his own people? Absolutely not! I am an Israelite myself — a descendant of Abraham, from the tribe of Benjamin. God has not rejected the people he chose from the very beginning. Don't you remember what the Scripture says about Elijah? He complained to God about Israel, saying, "Lord, they have killed your prophets and torn down your altars. I am the only one left, and now they are trying to kill me too."

But how did God answer him? "I have kept for myself seven thousand men who have never bowed down to worship the idol Baal." It's the same way today — there is a group of people that God has chosen by his grace. And since it is by grace, it isn't based on anything they've done. If it were based on works, grace wouldn't really be grace.

What Happened to the Rest of Israel?

So what happened? Most of Israel didn't find what they were looking for. Only the chosen ones found it, while the rest had their hearts hardened. As the Scripture says: "God gave them a spirit of deep sleep — eyes that don't see and ears that don't hear, right down to this very day." And David says: "Let their blessings become a trap and a snare — something that trips them up and brings them down. Let their eyes be darkened so they cannot see, and keep their backs bent under their burdens forever."

Salvation Has Come to the Gentiles

So I ask again — did Israel trip so badly that they fell forever? No! Because of their stumble, salvation has come to the Gentiles, and this was meant to make Israel jealous. Now if their stumble brought such riches to the world, and their failure brought such riches to the Gentiles, imagine how much greater the blessing will be when all of Israel is restored!

I am speaking especially to you Gentiles. Since I am an apostle to the Gentiles, I take my work seriously. I hope that somehow I might make my own people jealous and save some of them. For if God's setting them aside meant that the rest of the world was brought back to him, what will it be like when he accepts them back? It will be nothing less than life from the dead!

The Olive Tree

If the first piece of dough is holy, the whole batch is holy. If the root of a tree is holy, the branches are holy too. Now imagine some of the branches of an olive tree were broken off. You — like a wild olive branch — were grafted in among the others. Now you share in the rich life of the cultivated olive root. But don't brag about being better than the branches that were broken off. Remember, you don't support the root — the root supports you.

You might say, "Those branches were broken off so that I could be grafted in." That's true. They were broken off because they didn't believe, and you are there only because you do believe. So don't be arrogant — be grateful and humble. If God didn't spare the natural branches, he won't automatically spare you either.

Look at how God is both kind and severe. He is severe toward those who fell, but kind to you — as long as you keep living in his kindness. Otherwise you'll be cut off too. And if the people of Israel turn from their unbelief, God will graft them back in. He has the power to do it. After all, if you were cut from a wild olive tree and grafted onto a good one — which goes against nature — it will be even easier for God to graft the original branches back onto their own tree.

A Mystery Revealed

Brothers and sisters, I want you to understand this so that you don't become proud. Part of Israel has become stubborn, but this will last only until the full number of Gentiles has come to God. And then all Israel will be saved, as the Scripture says: "The Savior will come from Zion and remove all ungodliness from the family of Jacob. And this is my covenant with them — I will take away their sins."

As far as the good news goes, they are currently treated as enemies for your sake. But as far as God's choice goes, they are still loved because of the promises God made to their ancestors. God never changes his mind about the people he calls or the gifts he gives.

At one time you were disobedient to God, but now you have received mercy because of their disobedience. In the same way, they have now become disobedient so that they too might receive the same mercy you received. God has allowed everyone to fall into disobedience so that he can show mercy to everyone.

Praise to God

How great are God's riches and his wisdom and knowledge! No one can explain his decisions or understand his ways.

"Who has ever known the mind of the Lord? Who has ever been his advisor?"

"Who has ever given anything to God that God must pay back?"

Everything comes from him, exists through him, and is for him. To him be glory forever. Amen.

Romans Chapter 12

Give Yourself Completely to God

So brothers and sisters, because God has shown us such great mercy, I urge you to offer your lives as a living sacrifice to him — keep yourselves holy and do what pleases him. This is the truest form of worship there is. Don't copy the behaviors and customs of this world. Instead, let God transform you into a new person by changing the way you think. Then you'll learn to know God's will for you — which is good, pleasing, and perfect.

Use the Gifts God Gave You

Because of the grace God has given me, I have something to say to each of you: Don't think of yourself as more important than you really are. Be honest in how you see yourself, measured by the faith God has given you.

Just as our bodies have many parts — and each part has its own job — it is the same with the body of Christ. Though we are many, we are one body, and we all belong to each other. In his grace, God has given each of us different gifts for doing certain things well.

If your gift is prophecy, speak out with as much faith as God has given you. If your gift is serving others, serve them well. If you are a teacher, teach well. If your gift is encouraging others, be encouraging. If it is giving, give generously. If God has given you leadership ability, take that responsibility seriously. And if you have a gift for showing kindness, do it gladly.

How to Treat Each Other

Don't just pretend to love others — really love them. Hate what is wrong and hold tightly to what is good. Love each other with genuine affection and take delight in honoring each other. Never be lazy, but work hard and serve the Lord with an enthusiastic heart.

Rejoice in the hope you have. Be patient in trouble and keep on praying. When God's people are in need, help them. Always be eager to welcome others into your home.

Bless those who persecute you. Don't curse them — pray that God will bless them. Be happy with those who are happy, and weep with those who weep. Live in harmony with each other. Don't be too proud to enjoy the company of ordinary people. And don't think you know it all.

Overcome Evil with Good

Never pay back evil with more evil. Live in such a way that everyone can see you are honorable. Do everything you can to live in peace with everyone.

Dear friends, never take revenge. Leave that to God. For the Scripture says, "I will take revenge and pay them back," says the Lord. Instead, "If your enemies are hungry, feed them. If they are thirsty, give them something to drink. In doing this, you will heap burning coals of shame on their heads."

Don't let evil conquer you. Conquer evil by doing good.

Romans Chapter 13

Respect for Authorities

Everyone should respect and obey the governing authorities. There is no authority that hasn't been allowed by God — the authorities that exist have been put in place under his oversight. So anyone who rebels against authority is resisting what God has arranged, and those who do so will bring judgment on themselves.

Public officials are not a threat to people who do what is right — only to those who do wrong. Do you want to live without fear of the authorities? Then do what is good and they'll treat you well. The person in authority is God's servant, working for your benefit. But if you do what is wrong, you should be afraid — because they don't carry the power of punishment for no reason. They are God's servants, sent to bring justice to those who practice evil.

So obey — not just to avoid punishment, but because your conscience tells you it's the right thing to do. This is also why you pay taxes. The officials are God's servants, giving their full attention to these duties. Give everyone what you owe them: if you owe taxes, pay them. If you owe fees, pay them. If you owe respect, give it. If you owe honor, give it.

Love Fulfills the Law

Don't owe anyone anything — except the ongoing debt of loving one another. The person who loves others has fulfilled everything the law requires. The commandments — "Do not commit adultery," "Do not murder," "Do not steal," "Do not covet" — and every other command are all summed up in this one rule: "Love your neighbor as yourself." Love does no harm to a neighbor. So love is the complete fulfillment of the law.

Wake Up — the Day Is Coming

Do all of this because you know what time it is. It's time to wake up from your sleep. Our salvation is closer now than when we first believed. The night is almost over and the day is about to dawn. So let's get rid of the things we do in the dark and put on the armor of light.

Let's live decent lives — lives that can stand up to the daylight. No wild parties or drunkenness. No sexual immorality or shameful behavior. No fighting or jealousy. Instead, clothe yourselves with the Lord Jesus the Messiah and stop making plans to feed the selfish desires of your old nature.

Romans Chapter 14

Welcome Those Whose Faith Is Weak

Welcome anyone whose faith is weak, but don't start arguments with them over their personal opinions. One person's faith allows them to eat anything, while another person — whose faith is weak — eats only vegetables. The one who eats everything shouldn't look down on the one who doesn't, and the one who doesn't eat everything shouldn't judge the one who does — because God has accepted them both.

Who are you to judge someone else's servant? They answer to their own Master — not to you. And they will stand, because the Lord is able to make them stand.

In the same way, one person thinks certain days are more sacred than others, while someone else thinks every day is the same. Each person should be fully convinced in their own mind. Whoever observes a special day does it to honor the Lord. Whoever eats meat does it to honor the Lord and gives thanks to God. And whoever doesn't eat meat also does it to honor the Lord and gives thanks to God.

We All Belong to the Lord

None of us lives only for ourselves, and none of us dies only for ourselves. If we live, we live for the Lord. If we die, we die for the Lord. So whether we live or die, we belong to the Lord. This is why the Messiah died and came back to life — so that he might be Lord of both the dead and the living.

So why do you judge your brother or sister? Or why do you look down on them? We will all stand before God's judgment seat one day. As it is written:

"As surely as I live, says the Lord, every knee will bow before me and every tongue will acknowledge God."

So then, each of us will give an account of ourselves to God.

Don't Be a Stumbling Block

Let's stop judging each other. Instead, make up your mind not to put anything in the way of a brother or sister that would cause them to stumble. I am fully convinced by the Lord Jesus that no food is unclean in itself. But if someone believes something is unclean, then for that person it is unclean.

If your brother or sister is hurt because of what you eat, you are no longer acting out of love. Don't let your food choices destroy someone for whom the Messiah died. Don't let what you consider good become something others speak evil about.

The kingdom of God isn't about what you eat or drink — it's about doing right, living in peace, and finding joy in the Holy Spirit. Anyone who serves the Messiah this way is pleasing to God and respected by others.

Build Each Other Up

So let's aim for the things that bring peace and the things that help us build each other up. Don't tear down God's work over food. All food is clean, but it is wrong to eat something that causes someone else to stumble. It is better not to eat meat or drink wine or do anything else that causes your brother or sister to fall.

Keep your personal convictions about these things between yourself and God. Blessed is the person who doesn't feel guilty about what they've decided is right. But if someone has doubts and eats anyway, they are condemned — because they aren't acting from faith. And anything that doesn't come from faith is sin.

Romans Chapter 15

Put Others First

Those of us who are strong in our convictions ought to help carry the burdens of those who are weak. We shouldn't just live to please ourselves. Each of us should try to please our neighbor for their own good — to help build them up. Even the Messiah didn't live to please himself. As the Scripture says, "The insults of those who insulted you have fallen on me."

Everything written in the past was written to teach us, so that through endurance and the encouragement found in the Scriptures we might have hope. May the God who gives endurance and encouragement help you live in complete harmony with one another — following the example of Jesus the Messiah. Then with one mind and one voice you can give glory to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus the Messiah.

Welcome One Another

So welcome one another, just as the Messiah welcomed you, in order to bring glory to God. The Messiah became a servant to the Jewish people to prove that God is true and to confirm the promises made to their ancestors. He also did this so that the Gentiles could praise God for his mercy. As the Scriptures say:

"Because of this I will praise you among the nations — I will sing praises to your name."

And again: "Rejoice, you Gentiles, along with his people."

And: "Praise the Lord, all you Gentiles — let all the peoples praise him."

And Isaiah says: "The Root of Jesse will come — the one who rises to rule the nations. In him the nations will put their hope."

May the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace as you trust in him, so that you overflow with hope by the power of the Holy Spirit.

Paul's Work Among the Gentiles

Brothers and sisters, I am personally convinced that you are full of goodness, filled with knowledge, and more than capable of teaching one another. Even so, I have written to you quite boldly on some points — just to remind you of them. I do this because of the grace God gave me to be a servant of Jesus the Messiah to the Gentiles. I serve the good news of God like a priest, so that the Gentiles might become an acceptable offering to God, made holy by the Holy Spirit.

So in Christ Jesus I am proud of what God has done through me. I won't dare speak of anything except what the Messiah has accomplished through me — leading the Gentiles to obey God by what I have said and done, through the power of signs and wonders, and by the power of the Holy Spirit. From Jerusalem all the way around to Illyricum I have fully spread the good news of the Messiah.

My ambition has always been to preach the good news where the Messiah was not yet known — so I wouldn't be building on someone else's foundation. As it is written:

"Those who were never told about him will see, and those who have never heard will understand."

Paul's Plans to Visit Rome

This is why I have been kept from coming to see you so many times. But now, since I've finished my work in these regions and have longed to visit you for many years, I hope to see you on my way to Spain. I look forward to stopping in and visiting with you, and I hope you can help send me on my way after we've had some time together.

Right now I'm on my way to Jerusalem to serve the believers there. The believers in Macedonia and Achaia were glad to take up a collection for the poor among God's people in Jerusalem. They were happy to do it — and honestly, they owe it to them. If the Gentiles have shared in the spiritual blessings of the Jewish people, they ought to serve them in return with material help.

Once I've finished this task and made sure the gift has been delivered, I'll head to Spain and stop by to see you. And I know that when I come, I'll come with the full blessing of the Messiah.

A Request for Prayer

I urge you, brothers and sisters, by our Lord Jesus the Messiah and by the love of the Spirit — join me in my struggle by praying to God for me. Pray that I'll be kept safe from those in Judea who refuse to believe, and that my service in Jerusalem will be gladly received by the believers there. Then, if it is God's will, I'll be able to come to you with joy and find rest among you.

May the God of peace be with you all. Amen.

Romans Chapter 16

Phoebe Is Recommended

I want to introduce you to our sister Phoebe, who serves as a deacon of the church in Cenchreae. Please welcome her in the Lord in a way that is worthy of God's people, and give her whatever help she needs — because she has been a tremendous help and protector to many people, including me.

Greetings to Friends in Rome

Give my greetings to Prisca and Aquila, my co-workers in Christ Jesus. They risked their own lives to save mine, and I'm not the only one grateful for them — all the churches among the Gentiles are too. Give my greetings to the church that meets in their home.

Greet my dear friend Epaenetus, the first person in the province of Asia to follow the Messiah. Greet Mary, who worked very hard for you. Greet Andronicus and Junia, my fellow Jews and fellow prisoners — they are highly respected among the apostles and were followers of the Messiah even before I was.

Greet Ampliatus, whom I love in the Lord. Greet Urbanus, our co-worker in the Messiah, and my dear friend Stachys. Greet Apelles, whose faithfulness to the Messiah has been proven. Greet those who belong to the household of Aristobulus. Greet Herodion, my fellow countryman. Greet those in the household of Narcissus who belong to the Lord.

Greet Tryphaena and Tryphosa, who work hard for the Lord. Greet my dear friend Persis, who has also worked very hard for the Lord. Greet Rufus, a choice servant of the Lord, and his mother — who has been like a mother to me as well.

Greet Asyncritus, Phlegon, Hermes, Patrobas, Hermas, and the brothers and sisters with them. Greet Philologus, Julia, Nereus and his sister, Olympas, and all of God's people who are with them.

Greet one another with a holy kiss. All the churches of the Messiah send you their greetings.

Watch Out for Troublemakers

Brothers and sisters, I urge you to watch out for people who cause divisions and put obstacles in your way that go against the teaching you have received. Stay away from them. People like that are not serving our Lord the Messiah — they are serving their own appetites. With smooth talk and flattery they deceive people who don't know any better.

Everyone has heard about your obedience, and I couldn't be more glad. But I want you to be wise about what is good and completely innocent when it comes to evil. The God of peace will soon crush Satan under your feet. May the grace of our Lord Jesus be with you.

Greetings from Paul's Team

Timothy, my co-worker, sends his greetings. So do Lucius, Jason, and Sosipater, my fellow Jews.

I, Tertius — the one who actually wrote this letter down — greet you in the Lord.

Gaius, who has welcomed me and the whole church into his home, sends his greetings. Erastus, the city treasurer, and our brother Quartus send their greetings too.

A Final Word of Praise

Now to God — the only wise God — who has the power to make you strong. He does this through the good news I preach about Jesus the Messiah. For ages and ages this was a mystery, kept secret and hidden. But now it has been revealed. Through the words of the prophets, by the command of the eternal God himself, this mystery has been made known to all nations — so that people everywhere might hear and believe and obey.

To him be glory forever through Jesus the Messiah. Amen.
