2 Peter

2 Peter — (AENT) Accessible English New Testament

This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.

A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."

This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.

Grace & Peace.

2 Peter Chapter 1

Opening Greeting

Simon Peter, a servant and apostle of Jesus the Messiah — to those who have received a faith as precious as ours through the righteousness of our God and Savior Jesus the Messiah. May grace and peace be multiplied to you through the full knowledge of God and of Jesus our Master.

Everything We Need

His divine power has given us everything we need for life and true godliness through the knowledge of the one who called us by his own glory and excellence. Through these he has given us his great and precious promises — so that through them you might become partakers of the divine nature, having escaped the corruption in the world caused by evil desires.

Add to Your Faith

For this very reason make every effort to add to your faith excellence — and to excellence add knowledge. To knowledge add self-control — to self-control add endurance — to endurance add godliness. To godliness add brotherly affection — and to brotherly affection add love. For if you have these qualities and they are growing they will keep you from being useless or unproductive in your knowledge of our Master Jesus the Messiah. But anyone who lacks these things is blind and shortsighted — having forgotten that they were cleansed from their past sins.

So brothers and sisters be all the more diligent to make your calling and election certain. If you do these things you will never stumble — and you will be richly welcomed into the eternal kingdom of our Master and Savior Jesus the Messiah.

I Will Always Remind You

Because of this I will always remind you of these things — even though you already know them and are established in the truth you have. I think it is right as long as I am in this body to keep stirring you up by reminding you. I know that I will soon lay aside this earthly dwelling — just as our Master Jesus the Messiah made clear to me. And I will do my best to make sure that after my departure you will be able to call these things to mind at all times.

We Were Eyewitnesses

We were not following cleverly invented myths when we told you about the power and coming of our Master Jesus the Messiah — we were eyewitnesses of his majesty. He received honor and glory from God the Father when a voice came to him from the Majestic Glory saying —

This is my Son whom I love — I am well pleased with him.

We heard this voice ourselves coming from heaven while we were with him on the holy mountain.

The Prophetic Word

And we have the prophetic word made even more certain. You do well to pay attention to it — like a lamp shining in a dark place — until the day dawns and the morning star rises in your hearts. Most importantly you must understand that no prophecy of Scripture comes from a person's own interpretation. Prophecy never came about by human will — instead people spoke from God as they were carried along by the Holy Spirit.

2 Peter Chapter 2

False Teachers Will Come

Just as there were false prophets among the people in the past there will also be false teachers among you. They will secretly introduce destructive heresies — even denying the Master who bought them — and bring swift destruction on themselves. Many will follow their immoral ways and because of them the way of truth will be brought into disrepute. In their greed they will exploit you with made-up stories. Their judgment decreed long ago is not idle and their destruction is not asleep.

God Knows How to Rescue the Godly

For if God did not spare angels when they sinned but threw them into the deepest darkness keeping them in chains for judgment — and if he did not spare the ancient world but protected Noah, a preacher of righteousness, along with seven others when he brought a flood on a world of ungodly people — and if he condemned the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah to ashes making them an example of what is coming to the ungodly — and if he rescued righteous Lot who was distressed by the filthy conduct of lawless people, for that righteous man living among them day after day was tormented in his soul by the lawless deeds he saw and heard —

Then the Master certainly knows how to rescue the godly from trials and to keep the unrighteous under punishment until the day of judgment.

Bold and Arrogant — Like Irrational Animals

This is especially true for those who follow the corrupt desires of the flesh and despise authority. Bold and arrogant they are not afraid to insult heavenly beings — even though angels who are far stronger and more powerful do not bring insulting charges against such beings before the Master. But these people are like irrational animals — creatures of instinct born only to be caught and destroyed. They insult what they do not understand and in their corruption they will surely perish.

They will be paid back with harm for the harm they have done. They think it is pleasure to carouse in broad daylight. They are blots and blemishes reveling in their deceptions while they feast with you. Their eyes are full of adultery — never stopping sinning — enticing unstable souls. Their hearts are experts in greed. They are children under a curse.

They have left the straight path and wandered off to follow the way of Balaam son of Beor who loved the wages of wickedness. But he was rebuked for his wrongdoing — a speechless donkey spoke with a human voice and stopped the prophet's madness.

Dried Up Springs — Slaves to Corruption

These people are like dried up springs and mists driven by a storm — the deepest darkness has been reserved for them. By speaking arrogant and empty words they use fleshly desires and debauchery to entice people who are just escaping from those who live in error. They promise freedom while they themselves are slaves to corruption — for a person is a slave to whatever has conquered them.

For if after escaping the pollutions of the world through the knowledge of our Master and Savior Jesus the Messiah they are again entangled in them and defeated — their last condition is worse than the first. It would have been better for them never to have known the way of righteousness than to have known it and then turned their backs on the holy commandment delivered to them. What has happened to them proves the truth of the proverb —

A dog returns to its own vomit.

And —

A scrubbed pig goes back to wallowing in the mud.

2 Peter Chapter 3

Mockers Will Come

Dear friends — this is now the second letter I am writing to you. In both of them I am trying to stir up your sincere minds by reminding you of these things. I want you to remember the words spoken in the past by the holy prophets and the commandment of our Master and Savior given through your apostles.

First of all you must understand that in the last days mockers will come following their own desires and scoffing at the truth. They will say — where is this promise of his coming? Since the time our ancestors fell asleep everything has stayed exactly the same as it was from the very beginning of creation.

But they deliberately forget that long ago by the word of God the heavens existed and the earth was formed out of water and through water. By these same waters the world of that time was flooded and destroyed. And by that same word the present heavens and earth are being reserved for fire — kept for the day of judgment and the destruction of ungodly people.

One Day Is Like a Thousand Years

But do not forget this one thing dear friends — with the Master one day is like a thousand years and a thousand years are like one day. The Master is not slow about his promise as some people understand slowness. Instead he is being patient with you — not wanting anyone to perish but wanting everyone to come to repentance.

But the day of the Master will come like a thief. On that day the heavens will pass away with a roar — the elements will be dissolved by intense heat and the earth and everything done in it will be laid bare.

What Kind of People Ought You to Be

Since everything is going to be dissolved in this way — think about what kind of people you ought to be. Live holy and godly lives as you look forward to the day of God and eagerly await its coming. On that day the heavens will be set on fire and dissolved and the elements will melt in the heat. But based on his promise we are looking forward to a new heaven and a new earth where righteousness makes its home.

So dear friends — since you are looking forward to these things make every effort to be found at peace with him, without spot or blemish. Remember that the patience of our Master means salvation. Our dear brother Paul also wrote to you about this using the wisdom God gave him. He speaks about these things in all his letters — and some things in them are hard to understand, which ignorant and unstable people twist to their own destruction just as they do with the rest of the Scriptures.

Grow in Grace and Knowledge

So dear friends — since you already know these things stay on your guard so that you are not carried away by the errors of lawless people and lose your own secure footing. Instead grow in the grace and knowledge of our Master and Savior Jesus the Messiah.

To him be the glory both now and to the day of eternity. Amen.
