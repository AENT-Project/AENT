A Friendly, Plain American English Translation
(AENT) Accessible English New Testament
This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.
A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."
This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.

Jude
Opening Greeting
From Jude — a servant of Jesus the Messiah and brother of James — to those who have been called, who are loved by God the Father and kept safe for Jesus the Messiah. May mercy, peace, and love be given to you in abundance.
Contend for the Faith
Dear friends — although I was very eager to write to you about the salvation we all share I felt it was necessary to write and urge you to fight hard for the faith that was once and for all delivered to God's holy people. For certain people have slipped in among you unnoticed — people long ago designated for this judgment. They are ungodly people who turn the grace of our God into an excuse for blatant immorality and deny our only Master and Lord Jesus the Messiah.
I want to remind you — even though you already know all this — that although the Lord once saved a people out of the land of Egypt he later destroyed those who did not believe. And the angels who did not stay within their own position of authority but abandoned their proper home he has kept in eternal chains under darkness for the judgment of the great day. In the same way Sodom and Gomorrah and the surrounding cities which gave themselves over to sexual immorality and pursued unnatural desires serve as an example by undergoing the punishment of eternal fire.
These People Are Like —
Despite this these dreamers also defile their bodies, reject authority, and insult heavenly beings. Yet even Michael the archangel when he was arguing with the devil about the body of Moses did not dare to bring a slanderous charge against him — but simply said —
The Lord rebuke you.
But these people insult what they do not understand — and the things they do know by instinct like unthinking animals are the very things that destroy them.
Woe to them. They have followed the path of Cain — they have rushed headlong into Balaam's error for the sake of profit — and they have been destroyed in Korah's rebellion.
These people are like hidden reefs at your fellowship meals — eating with you without fear, caring only for themselves. They are clouds without rain blown along by the winds — autumn trees without fruit, twice dead and pulled up by the roots. They are wild waves of the sea foaming up their own shame — wandering stars for whom the deepest darkness has been reserved forever.
Enoch's Prophecy
Enoch the seventh from Adam prophesied about them saying —
Look — the Lord is coming with thousands upon thousands of his holy ones to execute judgment on everyone. He will convict all the ungodly for all the ungodly acts they have done in their ungodliness and for all the harsh things ungodly sinners have spoken against him.
These people are grumblers and complainers who follow their own evil desires — bragging about themselves and flattering others just to get what they want.
Remember What the Apostles Foretold
But you dear friends must remember what the apostles of our Lord Jesus the Messiah foretold. They told you — in the last times there will be scoffers who follow their own ungodly desires. These are the people who cause divisions — worldly-minded, not having the Spirit.
But you dear friends — build yourselves up in your most holy faith and pray in the Holy Spirit. Keep yourselves in the love of God as you wait for the mercy of our Lord Jesus the Messiah that leads to eternal life.
Be merciful to those who doubt. Save others by snatching them out of the fire. To others show mercy mixed with fear — hating even the clothing stained by corrupted flesh.
Closing Doxology
Now to him who is able to keep you from stumbling and to present you blameless before his glorious presence with great joy — to the only God our Savior through Jesus the Messiah our Lord be glory, majesty, power, and authority — before all time, now and forevermore. Amen.
