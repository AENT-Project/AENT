2 Thessalonians

A Friendly, Plain American English Translation
(AENT) Accessible English New Testament
This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.
A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."
This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.
Grace & Peace.


2 Thessalonians Chapter 1
Opening Greeting
Paul, Silvanus, and Timothy — to the church of the Thessalonians in God our Father and our Master Jesus the Messiah. Grace and peace to you from God our Father and from our Master Jesus the Messiah.
Your Faith Is Growing
We are always grateful to God for you brothers and sisters — as we should be. Your faith is growing remarkably and the love every one of you has for each other is overflowing. Because of this we boast about you among the churches of God — telling others of your endurance and faith through all the persecutions and hardships you are bearing.
This is clear evidence of God's righteous judgment — that you are being made worthy of the kingdom of God for which you are suffering. And it is right for God to pay back those who trouble you with trouble of their own.
The Return of the Master
To you who are being troubled he will give rest along with us — when the Master Jesus is revealed from heaven with his powerful angels. He will appear in flaming fire bringing judgment on those who don't know God and on those who refuse to obey the good news of our Master Jesus. These will pay the penalty of eternal separation from the presence of the Master and from the glory of his power.
This will happen on that day when he comes to be glorified among his people and to be marveled at by all who have believed — including you, because you believed our testimony.
Constant Prayer
With this in mind we always pray for you — asking that our God would make you worthy of his calling. We pray that by his power he would fulfill every good desire you have and every work that flows from your faith. We do this so that the name of our Master Jesus may be glorified in you and you in him — according to the grace of our God and the Master Jesus the Messiah.

2 Thessalonians Chapter 2
Don't Be Alarmed
Brothers and sisters — regarding the coming of our Master Jesus the Messiah and our gathering together to meet him, we ask you not to let your minds be easily unsettled or alarmed. Don't be shaken by any spirit, or by any message or letter supposedly coming from us, claiming that the day of the Master has already arrived.
Don't let anyone deceive you in any way. That day will not come until the great rebellion happens first and the man of lawlessness is revealed — the one destined for destruction. He opposes and exalts himself above everything that is called God or is worshiped. He will even go so far as to sit in the temple of God and declare himself to be God.
Don't you remember that I told you these things when I was still with you? You know what is holding him back so that he may be revealed at the proper time. The secret power of lawlessness is already at work — but the one who holds it back will continue to do so until he is taken out of the way.
The Defeat of the Lawless One
Then the lawless one will be revealed. The Master Jesus will strike him down with the breath of his mouth and destroy him by the splendor of his coming. This lawless one comes by the working of Satan with all kinds of counterfeit power, signs, and miracles. He will use every kind of wicked deception to fool those who are perishing — because they refused to love the truth and so be saved.
For this reason God sends them a powerful delusion so that they will believe the lie. And as a result all who refused to believe the truth and instead delighted in wickedness will be condemned.
Stand Firm
But we must always thank God for you brothers and sisters loved by the Master. We thank him because God chose you from the beginning for salvation through the sanctifying work of the Spirit and faith in the truth. He called you to this through our good news so that you might share in the glory of our Master Jesus the Messiah.
So then brothers and sisters — stand firm and hold tightly to what we passed on to you whether by word of mouth or by letter.
A Prayer for Comfort
May our Master Jesus the Messiah himself and God our Father — who loved us and by his grace gave us eternal comfort and a wonderful hope — encourage your hearts and strengthen you in every good thing you do and say.

2 Thessalonians Chapter 3
Pray for Us
Finally brothers and sisters — pray for us. Pray that the word of the Master may spread rapidly and be honored just as it was among you. And pray that we may be rescued from wicked and evil people — for not everyone has faith.
But the Master is faithful. He will strengthen you and protect you from the evil one. We are confident in the Master that you are doing and will continue to do what we ask. May the Master lead your hearts into the love of God and into the patient endurance of the Messiah.
Warning Against Idleness
Now we command you brothers and sisters — in the name of our Master Jesus the Messiah — to keep away from every brother or sister who lives an undisciplined life and doesn't follow what you received from us.
You yourselves know how you ought to follow our example. We were not idle when we were with you. We didn't eat anyone's food without paying for it. Instead we worked hard night and day so we wouldn't be a financial burden to any of you. We did this not because we don't have the right to support — but to give you an example to follow.
Even when we were with you we gave you this rule — if anyone is unwilling to work they shall not eat.
We hear that some among you are living undisciplined lives. They aren't busy working — they are busybodies interfering in other people's business. In the name of the Master Jesus the Messiah we command and encourage such people to settle down, work quietly, and earn their own living.
Final Blessing
As for the rest of you brothers and sisters — never get tired of doing what is good. If anyone doesn't obey what we say in this letter take note of that person. Don't associate with them so that they may feel ashamed — but don't treat them as an enemy. Warn them as a brother or sister.
May the Master of peace himself give you peace at all times and in every way. The Master be with you all.
I Paul write this greeting with my own hand — this is my distinguishing mark in all my letters. This is how I write.
The grace of our Master Jesus the Messiah be with you all.

Grace & Peace.
