Titus
A Friendly, Plain American English Translation
(AENT) Accessible English New Testament
This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.
A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."
This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.
Grace & Peace.

Titus Chapter 1
Opening Greeting
Paul, a servant of God and an apostle of Jesus the Messiah — sent to strengthen the faith of God's chosen people and to help them fully understand the truth that leads to a godly life. This faith and knowledge rest on the hope of eternal life — a life that God, who never lies, promised before time even began. And now at just the right time he has revealed his message through the preaching entrusted to me by the command of God our Savior.
To Titus, my true son in the faith we share — grace and peace to you from God the Father and from Jesus the Messiah our Savior.
Qualifications for Leaders
The reason I left you in Crete was so you could finish what still needed doing and appoint elders in every town just as I directed you. An elder must be someone no one can validly accuse of wrongdoing — faithful to his wife, with children who are believers and not known for being wild or rebellious.
Since a church leader manages God's household he must be blameless. Not arrogant, not quick-tempered, not a heavy drinker, not violent, not greedy for money. Instead he must be hospitable, a lover of what is good, sensible, fair, holy, and self-controlled. He must hold firmly to the trustworthy message as it was taught — so that he can encourage others with sound teaching and show those who oppose it where they are wrong.
Silence the False Teachers
For there are many rebellious people — especially those from the circumcision group — who talk nonsense and deceive others. They must be silenced because they are ruining entire families by teaching things they shouldn't, all for the sake of dishonest money. Even one of their own prophets said — Cretans are always liars, evil brutes, and lazy gluttons.
That statement is true. So correct them sharply so they can become sound in their faith and stop paying attention to Jewish myths and the man-made rules of people who have turned away from the truth.
To those who are pure everything is pure. But to those who are corrupt and unbelieving nothing is pure — their minds and consciences are both stained. They claim to know God but the way they live proves they don't. They are detestable, disobedient, and useless for anything good.

Titus Chapter 2
Teach Sound Doctrine to Everyone
You must teach what is consistent with sound doctrine. Teach the older men to be temperate, serious, and sensible — sound in faith, in love, and in patient endurance. Teach the older women to live in a way that reflects holiness — not slanderers, not enslaved to wine, but teachers of what is good. In this way they can train the younger women to love their husbands and children, to be self-controlled and pure, to manage their homes well, to be kind, and to be subject to their own husbands — so that the word of God will not be spoken against.
Encourage the young men to be self-controlled. In everything set them an example by doing what is good. In your teaching show integrity, seriousness, and soundness of speech that cannot be condemned — so that those who oppose you will be ashamed because they have nothing bad to say about us.
Teach servants to be subject to their masters in everything — to try to please them, not to talk back, and not to steal from them. Show that they can be fully trusted so that in every way they make the teaching about God our Savior attractive.
The Grace of God Has Appeared
For the grace of God has appeared — bringing salvation to all people. It teaches us to say no to ungodliness and worldly passions and to live self-controlled, upright, and godly lives in this present age — while we wait for the blessed hope, the appearing of the glory of our great God and Savior Jesus the Messiah. He gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good.
Teach these things. Encourage and correct with full authority. Don't let anyone look down on you.

Titus Chapter 3
Live Respectfully Among Everyone
Remind them to submit to rulers and authorities, to be obedient, and to be ready for every good work. Tell them to slander no one, to avoid fighting, to be gentle, and to show courtesy to everyone. For we ourselves were once foolish, disobedient, led astray, and enslaved to all kinds of lusts and pleasures. We lived in malice and envy — hated by others and hating one another.
Saved by Mercy Not by Works
But when the kindness and love of God our Savior appeared he saved us — not because of any righteous things we had done but because of his mercy. He saved us through the washing of rebirth and renewal by the Holy Spirit whom he poured out on us richly through Jesus the Messiah our Savior. This was done so that having been justified by his grace we might become heirs with the hope of eternal life.
This is a trustworthy saying — and I want you to insist on these things so that those who have believed in God may be careful to devote themselves to good works. These things are excellent and profitable for everyone. But avoid foolish controversies, genealogies, quarrels, and arguments about the law — they are unprofitable and worthless. After a first and second warning have nothing more to do with someone who causes division — knowing that such a person is warped and sinful and condemned by their own actions.
Final Instructions and Greetings
When I send Artemas or Tychicus to you do your best to come to me at Nicopolis — I have decided to spend the winter there. Do your best to help Zenas the lawyer and Apollos on their journey and make sure they have everything they need. Our people must also learn to devote themselves to good works to meet urgent needs so that they will not be unfruitful.
Everyone here sends their greetings. Greet those who love us in the faith. Grace be with you all.
