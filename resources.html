What This Project Is

The AENT — Accessible English New Testament — is a plain language translation of the entire New Testament, built to be readable by anyone regardless of education level. No archaic language. No difficult vocabulary. Just the word of God made clear.

The entire project — translation, website, and publishing — was completed using free tools that anyone can access. Our goal is to share exactly how we did it so teams around the world can do the same in their own languages.

The Source Text

The foundation of any NT translation project is a reliable Greek source text. We recommend starting with the publicly available Nestle-Aland Greek New Testament or the SBL Greek New Testament — both available free online. Search for "SBL Greek New Testament free PDF" to find it.

AI Tools We Recommend

For translation work — Claude (Anthropic) handles nuance, tone, and plain language rendering with exceptional care for meaning. This is the tool we used for the AENT translation.

For theological and technical questions — Claude and Gemini (Google) are both strong for deep theological questions, word studies, and translation debates.

For image generation — ChatGPT (OpenAI) produces excellent logos, illustrations, and visual assets. Our AENT logo was made with ChatGPT.

For website building and coding — Claude. Give it your chapter text and it will build clean HTML pages ready to publish.

Free Publishing Tools


Google Docs — free cloud-based word processor from Google. This is where you write, store, and polish your translation chapter by chapter. Your work is automatically saved, accessible from any device, and never lost. When a chapter is ready, simply copy it from Google Docs and paste it directly into SeaMonkey Composer — the formatting transfers cleanly. Sign up free at docs.google.com.


Gmail — free email from Google. Set up a dedicated project email address for your translation ministry — just like we did with aentproject@gmail.com. This gives you a professional contact address separate from your personal email, and since Gmail and Google Docs share the same Google account you only need to sign up once to get both. Sign up free at gmail.com.

SeaMonkey Composer — a free WYSIWYG HTML editor. Works like Microsoft Word. Paste your translated text, apply headings, save as HTML. Download free at seamonkey-project.org.

GitHub Pages — free website hosting for anyone. Upload your HTML files and your translation is live on the internet instantly, for free, forever. Sign up at github.com.

The AENT HTML Template — full methodology guide and downloadable template coming soon. Contact us to receive it early.
