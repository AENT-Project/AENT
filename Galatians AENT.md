Galatians
A Friendly, Plain American English Translation
(AENT) Accessible English New Testament
This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.
A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."
This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.
Grace & Peace.

Galatians Chapter 1
Opening Greeting
Paul, an apostle — not appointed by any committee of men or by any human authority, but by Jesus the Messiah and God the Father who raised him from the dead — and all the brothers with me, to the churches in Galatia.
Grace and peace to you from God our Father and our Master Jesus the Messiah, who gave himself up for our sins to rescue us from this present evil age, exactly as God our Father intended. To him be the glory forever and ever. Amen.
No Other Gospel
I am honestly shocked that you are turning away so quickly from the One who called you by the grace of the Messiah and chasing after a different kind of good news — which really isn't good news at all. There are people throwing you into confusion, trying to twist and corrupt the good news of the Messiah.
But listen — even if we, or an angel straight from heaven, should preach a good news that contradicts what we already preached to you, let that person be cursed. I'll say it again just as we said before — if anyone preaches a version of the good news to you that is different from what you first received, let them be cursed.
Does it sound like I'm trying to win human approval? Am I just trying to please people? If I were still trying to please people I wouldn't be a servant of the Messiah.
Paul's Gospel Came Directly from the Messiah
Brothers, I want you to know for a fact that the good news I preached isn't some human invention. I didn't get it from a man and nobody sat me down to teach it to me. I received it through a direct revelation from Jesus the Messiah.
You've heard about my former life in Judaism — how I obsessively persecuted the church of God and tried to destroy it. I was advancing in Judaism faster than most people my age because I was extremely passionate about the traditions of my ancestors.
But when God — who set me apart before I was even born and called me by his grace — was pleased to reveal his Son in me so that I could preach him among the nations, I didn't go out and consult with any human being. I didn't even go up to Jerusalem to see the apostles who were there before me. Instead I went off to Arabia and later came back to Damascus.
Paul Visits Jerusalem
Then three years later I finally went up to Jerusalem to visit Peter and stayed with him for fifteen days. I didn't see any of the other apostles except James, the Master's brother. I am telling you the truth before God — I am not lying about any of this.
After that I went into the regions of Syria and Cilicia. At that time the churches in Judea that belong to the Messiah didn't know me by sight. They only kept hearing the report — the man who used to persecute us is now preaching the very faith he tried to destroy. And they praised God because of what happened to me.

Galatians Chapter 2
Paul Returns to Jerusalem
Fourteen years later I went back up to Jerusalem with Barnabas and took Titus along with me. I went because of a direct revelation from God. While I was there I laid out the good news that I preach among the nations — doing this privately with those considered to be the leaders, because I wanted to be absolutely sure I wasn't running — or hadn't already run — my race for nothing.
But even Titus, who was with me and is a Greek, wasn't forced to be circumcised. This issue only came up because of some fake brothers who had been smuggled in. They slipped in like spies to snoop on the freedom we have in the Messiah, hoping to shove us back into slavery. We didn't give in to them for a single second — because we wanted to make sure the truth of the good news stayed solid for you.
The Pillars of the Church
As for those who were considered the important leaders — honestly, what they were makes no difference to me because God doesn't play favorites based on human status — those leaders didn't add a single thing to my message. On the contrary they saw that I had been entrusted with the good news for the Gentiles just as Peter had been entrusted with it for the Jews. For the same God who was at work in Peter as an apostle to the Jews was also at work in me for the Gentiles.
When James, Peter, and John — recognized as the pillars of the church — saw the grace that had been given to me, they gave Barnabas and me the right hand of fellowship. We agreed that we would go to the Gentiles and they would go to the Jews. The only thing they asked was that we remember the poor — the very thing I was already eager to do.
Paul Confronts Peter
But when Peter came to Antioch I had to confront him to his face because he was clearly in the wrong. Before certain men came from James he used to eat freely with the Gentiles. But once those men arrived he started pulling back and separating himself because he was afraid of the circumcision group. The rest of the Jewish believers joined him in this hypocrisy — and even Barnabas got swept along with them.
When I saw that they weren't walking a straight line according to the truth of the good news I said to Peter in front of everyone — if you, a Jew, have been living like a Gentile and not like a Jew, how can you now try to force the Gentiles to live like Jews?
Made Right by Faith, Not the Law
We are Jews by birth — not sinners from the Gentile world. Yet we know that a person is not made right with God by keeping the law, but through faith in Jesus the Messiah. So we too have put our trust in Jesus the Messiah so that we might be made right through faith in him and not by keeping the law — because no human being will ever be made right with God by keeping the law.
Now if we are seeking to be made right in the Messiah but are found to be sinners ourselves, does that make the Messiah a promoter of sin? Absolutely not! If I start rebuilding the very system I tore down I prove myself to be a lawbreaker. Through the law I died to the law so that I might live for God.
I have been crucified with the Messiah. It is no longer I who live — but the Messiah who lives in me. The life I am now living in this body I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave himself up for me. I am not throwing away the grace of God — because if being right with God comes through the law, then the Messiah died for absolutely nothing.

Galatians Chapter 3
You Foolish Galatians
You foolish Galatians! Who has cast a spell on you? Right before your very eyes Jesus the Messiah was clearly portrayed as crucified. I only want to know one thing — did you receive the Spirit by keeping the law or by hearing and believing the good news? Are you really this foolish? After starting your journey in the Spirit are you now trying to finish it by human effort? Have you gone through so much for nothing? Does God give you the Spirit and work miracles among you because you keep the law or because you hear and believe?
The Faith of Abraham
Consider Abraham — he believed God and it was credited to him as righteousness. So understand this — those who live by faith are the true children of Abraham. The Scripture saw this coming, that God would make the Gentiles right through faith, and announced the good news to Abraham ahead of time, saying "All the nations will be blessed through you." So those who live by faith are blessed along with Abraham, the man of faith.
On the other hand everyone who relies on keeping the law is under a curse. For it is written — "Cursed is everyone who does not keep on doing everything written in the Book of the Law." It's clear that no one is made right with God by the law because "the righteous person will live by faith." But the law isn't based on faith — instead it says "the person who does these things will live by them." The Messiah bought us back from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us — because it is written "cursed is everyone who is hanged on a tree." He did this so that the blessing given to Abraham would come to the Gentiles through the Messiah, and so that we might receive the promised Spirit through faith.
The Promise and the Law
Brothers, let me give you an everyday example. Even with a human contract — once it is officially signed nobody can just cancel it or add new conditions to it. Now the promises were spoken to Abraham and to his descendant. It doesn't say "and to descendants" referring to many, but "and to your descendant" referring to one person — who is the Messiah. What I'm saying is this — the law, which came four hundred and thirty years later, does not cancel the agreement God previously confirmed, and it doesn't do away with the promise. If the inheritance is based on the law it's no longer based on a promise — but God graciously gave it to Abraham through a promise.
So why was the law given? It was added because of sin until the promised descendant should come. It was put in place through angels by a mediator. A mediator isn't needed for just one party — but God is one.
Is the law then against the promises of God? Absolutely not! If a law had been given that could produce life then righteousness really would have come through the law. But the Scripture locked everything up under the power of sin so that what was promised through faith in Jesus the Messiah might be given to those who believe.
The Law Was Our Guardian
Before faith in the Messiah arrived we were held in custody by the law, locked up until this faith was revealed. The law was our guardian leading us to the Messiah so that we might be made right through faith. But now that faith has come we are no longer under a guardian.
All One in the Messiah
You are all children of God through faith in Jesus the Messiah. All of you who were baptized into the Messiah have clothed yourselves with the Messiah. There is no longer Jew or Gentile, slave or free, male or female — you are all one in Jesus the Messiah. And if you belong to the Messiah then you are Abraham's descendants and heirs according to the promise.

Galatians Chapter 4
Children and Heirs
Let me explain it this way — as long as an heir is a child he is no different from a slave, even though he actually owns everything. He is under the control of guardians and managers until the date his father sets. It was the same with us — when we were like children we were enslaved to the basic spiritual forces of this world. But when the right time came God sent his Son, born of a woman, born under the law, to buy back those who were under the law so that we might receive the full rights of his children.
And because you are his children God sent the Spirit of his Son into our hearts crying out "Abba, Father!" So you are no longer a slave but a child — and if you are a child then you are an heir through God.
Don't Go Back to Slavery
Back when you didn't know God you were slaves to things that aren't even gods. But now that you know God — or rather now that God knows you — how can you turn back to those weak and useless forces? Do you really want to be enslaved by them all over again? You are carefully observing special days, months, seasons, and years. I am genuinely afraid for you — worried that all my hard work for you might have been for nothing.
Paul's Plea
Brothers, I beg you — become like me because I became like you. You haven't wronged me. You know it was because of a physical illness that I first preached the good news to you. Even though my condition was difficult for you to deal with you didn't look down on me or turn me away. You welcomed me as if I were an angel of God — or even the Messiah himself.
So what happened to that joy you had? I can tell you that if it had been possible you would have torn out your own eyes and given them to me. Have I now become your enemy just because I'm telling you the truth?
Those people are working hard to win you over but not for any good reason. They want to cut you off from us so that you will follow them instead. It's always good to be pursued for good reasons — not just when I'm there with you. My dear children, I am going through the pains of childbirth for you all over again until the Messiah is fully formed in you. I wish I could be with you right now and change my tone because I am honestly at a loss about what is happening with you.
Two Covenants
Tell me — you who want to be under the law, do you even hear what the law says? It is written that Abraham had two sons, one by a slave woman and one by a free woman. The son of the slave woman was born by human effort, but the son of the free woman was born because of a promise.
This is an allegory. These two women represent two covenants. One covenant comes from Mount Sinai and gives birth to children who are slaves — that is Hagar. Hagar represents Mount Sinai in Arabia and corresponds to the present-day Jerusalem, because she and her children are in slavery. But the Jerusalem above is free — and she is our mother. For it is written:
"Rejoice, you woman who cannot have children! Shout for joy, you who have never gone into labor! Because the children of the deserted woman are more than the children of the woman who has a husband."
Children of the Promise
Now you, brothers and sisters, like Isaac, are children of the promise. But just as the son born by human effort persecuted the son born by the Spirit back then, so it is now. But what does the Scripture say? "Throw out the slave woman and her son, for the slave woman's son will never share the inheritance with the free woman's son." So then brothers and sisters — we are not children of the slave woman but of the free woman.

Galatians Chapter 5
Stand Firm in Freedom
The Messiah has set us free so that we can truly live in freedom — so stand firm and don't let yourselves be tied up again by a yoke of slavery. Listen to what I, Paul, am telling you — if you get circumcised to earn your salvation the Messiah will be of no benefit to you at all. I'll say it again to every man who gets circumcised for that reason — you are then obligated to keep the entire law perfectly. If you are trying to be made right with God by the law you have cut yourself off from the Messiah and fallen away from grace.
But for us — through the Spirit and by faith — we eagerly wait for the righteousness we hope for. In the Messiah neither circumcision nor the lack of it means anything. What matters is faith expressing itself through love.
Don't Let Anyone Cut In
You were running the race so well. Who cut in on you and kept you from obeying the truth? That kind of persuasion doesn't come from the One who calls you. Just a little yeast works its way through the whole batch of dough. I have confidence in you through our Master that you won't see it any other way. But whoever is throwing you into confusion will face the consequences.
Brothers, if I am still preaching that circumcision is necessary why am I still being persecuted? If I were doing that the offense of the cross would have been eliminated. I wish the people who are agitating you would go all the way and cut themselves off completely!
Freedom Through Love
You were called to be free, brothers and sisters. But don't use your freedom as an excuse to indulge your old sinful nature — instead serve one another humbly in love. The entire law is fulfilled in one command — "Love your neighbor as yourself." But if you keep biting and devouring each other watch out or you will destroy one another.
Walk by the Spirit
So I say — let the Spirit guide your lives and you won't satisfy the cravings of your sinful nature. The sinful nature wants what is contrary to the Spirit and the Spirit wants what is contrary to the sinful nature. They are in constant conflict with each other so that you end up not doing what you really want to do. But if you are led by the Spirit you are not under the law.
The Acts of the Sinful Nature
The results of the sinful nature are obvious — sexual immorality, impurity, indecency, idolatry, witchcraft, hatred, fighting, jealousy, fits of anger, selfish ambition, divisions, factions, envy, drunkenness, wild parties, and things like these. I am warning you now just as I did before — those who live like this will not inherit the kingdom of God.
The Fruit of the Spirit
But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control. There is no law against any of these. Those who belong to the Messiah have crucified their sinful nature along with its passions and desires. Since we live by the Spirit let us keep in step with the Spirit. Let us not become conceited — provoking one another or envying each other.

Galatians Chapter 6
Carry Each Other's Burdens
Brothers and sisters, if someone is caught doing something wrong, those of you who are guided by the Spirit should gently help that person get back on the right path. But watch yourself, so that you aren't tempted too. Carry each other's burdens — in this way you will fulfill the law of the Messiah. If anyone thinks they are something special when they are not they are only fooling themselves. Each person should examine their own actions and take pride in their own work without comparing themselves to anyone else — because everyone has their own load to carry.
Those who are taught the Word should share all good things with their teacher.
You Reap What You Plant
Don't be fooled — you can't make a fool out of God. Whatever a person plants that is exactly what they will harvest. If you plant to satisfy your sinful nature you will harvest decay and death. But if you plant to please the Spirit you will harvest eternal life. So let's not get tired of doing what is good — at just the right time we will harvest a blessing if we don't give up. So whenever we have the opportunity let's do good to everyone, and especially to those who belong to the family of faith.
The Only Thing Worth Bragging About
Look at these large letters I am writing to you with my own hand. Those who are trying to force you to be circumcised are only doing it to look good and avoid being persecuted for the cross of the Messiah. Even those who are circumcised don't keep the law themselves — they just want you circumcised so they can brag about it.
But as for me — may I never brag about anything except the cross of our Master Jesus the Messiah. Through him the world has been crucified to me and I have been crucified to the world. It doesn't matter whether a person is circumcised or not — what matters is being a new creation. May peace and mercy rest on everyone who lives by this truth, and on the Israel of God.
Final Words
From now on let no one cause me trouble — I carry the marks of Jesus on my own body.
Brothers and sisters, may the grace of our Master Jesus the Messiah be with your spirit. Amen.
