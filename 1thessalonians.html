1 Thessalonians

1 Thessalonians — (AENT) Accessible English New Testament

This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.

A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."

This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.

Grace & Peace.

1 Thessalonians Chapter 1

Opening Greeting

Paul, Silvanus, and Timothy — to the church of the Thessalonians in God the Father and our Master Jesus the Messiah. Grace and peace to you.

Faith, Love, and Hope

We always give thanks to God for all of you and constantly mention you in our prayers. We never forget how your faith has translated into action, how your love has driven you to hard work, and how your hope in our Master Jesus the Messiah has given you such unwavering endurance before our God and Father.

The Power of the Good News

Brothers and sisters loved by God — we know that he has chosen you. When we brought the good news to you it wasn't just a matter of words. It came with power, with the Holy Spirit, and with deep conviction. You saw how we lived among you and you know it was all for your benefit.

You Became an Example

And you became imitators of us and of the Master. Even though you faced great suffering you welcomed the Word with the joy that comes from the Holy Spirit. Because of this you became a model for all the believers throughout Macedonia and Achaia.

Turning from Idols to the Living God

The word of the Master has echoed out from you — not only throughout Macedonia and Achaia but your faith in God has become known everywhere. We don't even need to say anything about it because people everywhere are already telling the story of how you welcomed us. They talk about how you turned away from idols to serve the living and true God — and how you are now waiting for his Son from heaven, the one he raised from the dead — Jesus, who rescues us from the coming judgment.

1 Thessalonians Chapter 2

Our Visit Was Not Wasted

Brothers and sisters you know for yourselves that our visit to you was not a waste of time. Even though we had already suffered and been treated shamefully in Philippi — as you well know — we found the courage through our God to tell you the good news even in the face of strong opposition.

Our appeal to you doesn't come from error, impure motives, or any attempt to trick you. On the contrary we speak as those who have been approved by God and entrusted with the good news. We aren't trying to please people — we are trying to please God, who examines our very hearts.

Gentle as a Nursing Mother

We never used flattery and we never put on a mask to hide greed — God is our witness. We weren't looking for praise from people — not from you or anyone else — even though as apostles of the Messiah we could have made demands on you. Instead we were gentle among you like a nursing mother tenderly caring for her own children. We loved you so much that we were delighted to share with you not only the good news of God but our very lives as well — that is how dear you became to us.

Surely you remember brothers and sisters our hard work and toil. We worked night and day so we would not be a financial burden to any of you while we preached the good news of God. You are witnesses — and so is God — of how holy, righteous, and blameless our conduct was toward you. You know that we treated each of you as a father treats his own children — encouraging, comforting, and urging you to live lives worthy of God, who calls you into his own kingdom and glory.

You Received It as God's Word

We also constantly thank God because when you received the word we brought you accepted it not as a human message but as what it truly is — the word of God, which is powerfully at work in you who believe. For you brothers and sisters became imitators of the churches of God in Judea that belong to the Messiah Jesus. You suffered from your own countrymen the same things those churches suffered from the Jewish leaders — who killed both the Master Jesus and the prophets and who drove us out. They don't please God and are hostile to everyone — trying to keep us from speaking to the Gentiles so that they might be saved. In this way they keep piling up their sins. But judgment has caught up with them at last.

Paul's Longing to See Them

Brothers and sisters when we were torn away from you for a short time — in person but never in heart — we were all the more eager to see you face to face because of our intense longing for you. We wanted to come to you — I Paul tried more than once — but Satan blocked our way. For what is our hope, our joy, or the crown we will boast in before our Master Jesus when he returns? Is it not you?

Yes — you are our glory and our joy.

1 Thessalonians Chapter 3

Timothy's Mission

When we couldn't stand it any longer we decided it was best to stay behind in Athens alone and send Timothy to you. He is our brother and a fellow worker for God in the good news of the Messiah. We sent him to strengthen you and encourage you in your faith so that no one would be shaken by these troubles. You yourselves know that we are destined for this. In fact when we were with you we kept warning you ahead of time that we were going to face persecution — and as you know that is exactly what happened. That is why when I couldn't stand it any longer I sent Timothy to find out about your faith. I was afraid that the Tempter might have somehow gotten to you and that all our hard work would have been for nothing.

The Good News Timothy Brought

But Timothy has just now come back to us from visiting you and he brought wonderful news about your faith and love. He told us that you always have good memories of us and that you long to see us just as much as we long to see you. Because of this brothers and sisters — in the middle of all our own distress and trouble — your faith encouraged us. For now we truly feel alive knowing that you are standing firm in our Master.

How can we thank God enough for you — for all the joy we feel because of you in the presence of our God? Night and day we pray earnestly that we might see you face to face and supply whatever is still missing from your faith.

A Prayer for the Thessalonians

May our God and Father himself and our Master Jesus clear the way for us to come to you. May the Master make your love grow and overflow for each other and for everyone else just as our love overflows for you. And may he strengthen your hearts so that you will be blameless and holy in the presence of our God and Father when our Master Jesus returns with all his holy ones.

Amen.

1 Thessalonians Chapter 4

Live to Please God

Finally brothers and sisters — we ask and encourage you in the Master Jesus. You learned from us how you ought to live and please God and we know you are already doing this — but we urge you to do so even more. You know the instructions we gave you by the authority of the Master Jesus.

This is the will of God — that you live holy lives. Stay away from sexual immorality. Each of you should learn to control your own body in a way that is holy and honorable — not in lustful passion like people who don't know God. No one should take advantage of or cheat a brother or sister in this matter because the Master punishes all such sins — as we warned you before. God did not call us to live impure lives but to live holy lives. So anyone who rejects this instruction isn't rejecting a human being — they are rejecting God himself, who gives you his Holy Spirit.

Love Each Other More and More

Now about loving your brothers and sisters — we don't even need to write to you about this because God himself has taught you to love each other. And you are already showing this love to all the brothers and sisters throughout Macedonia. But we urge you to do it even more. Make it your ambition to live quietly, mind your own business, and work with your hands just as we told you. This way you will live respectably in the eyes of outsiders and won't need to depend on anyone.

The Return of the Master

Brothers and sisters we don't want you to be uninformed about those who have died — so that you don't grieve like people who have no hope. For if we believe that Jesus died and rose again we also believe that God will bring back with Jesus those who have died in him.

We tell you this directly from the Master — we who are still alive when the Master returns will certainly not go ahead of those who have already died. For the Master himself will come down from heaven with a commanding shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the blast of God's trumpet. Those who died in the Messiah will rise first. Then we who are still alive will be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Master in the air. And so we will be with the Master forever.

So encourage one another with these words.

1 Thessalonians Chapter 5

The Day of the Master

Brothers and sisters — regarding the times and dates you don't need us to write anything to you. You know very well that the day of the Master comes like a thief in the night. While people are saying "peace and security" sudden destruction will come upon them like labor pains on a pregnant woman — and they will not escape.

But you brothers and sisters are not in darkness so that this day should surprise you like a thief. You are all children of the light and children of the day. We don't belong to the night or to the darkness. So then let's not sleep like everyone else — let's stay awake and clear-headed. Those who sleep, sleep at night. Those who get drunk, get drunk at night. But since we belong to the day let's stay sober — putting on faith and love as a breastplate and the hope of salvation as a helmet.

God did not appoint us to face his judgment but to receive salvation through our Master Jesus the Messiah. He died for us so that whether we are awake or asleep we may live together with him. So encourage one another and build each other up — just as you are already doing.

How to Live Together

Brothers and sisters we ask you to recognize those who work hard among you — who care for you in the Master and who correct you when needed. Hold them in the highest regard in love because of their work. Live in peace with each other. And we urge you — warn those who are idle and disruptive, encourage the disheartened, help the weak, and be patient with everyone.

Make sure nobody pays back wrong for wrong — always try to do what is good for each other and for everyone else.

Short and Sharp

Always be joyful. Never stop praying. Give thanks in all circumstances — this is God's will for you in the Messiah Jesus.

Don't put out the Spirit's fire. Don't treat prophecies with contempt — but test everything carefully. Hold on to what is good and stay away from every kind of evil.

Closing Blessing

May God himself — the God of peace — make you completely holy. May your whole spirit, soul, and body be kept blameless at the coming of our Master Jesus the Messiah. The one who calls you is faithful — and he will do it.

Brothers and sisters pray for us too. Greet all the brothers and sisters with a holy kiss. I urge you before the Master to have this letter read to everyone.

The grace of our Master Jesus the Messiah be with you. Amen.
