1 Corinthians

1 Corinthians — (AENT) Accessible English New Testament

This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.

A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."

This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.

Grace & Peace.

1 Corinthians Chapter 1

Greeting to the Church in Corinth

From Paul, called by the will of God to be an apostle of Jesus the Messiah, and from our brother Sosthenes. To the church of God in Corinth — to those who have been set apart in Jesus the Messiah and called to be his holy people. This includes everyone everywhere who calls on the name of our Master, Jesus the Messiah, who is both their Master and ours. May grace and peace be yours from God our Father and from the Master Jesus the Messiah.

Thankful for the Corinthians

I always thank my God for you because of the grace he gave you through Christ Jesus. In him you've been made rich in every way — in your ability to speak and in your knowledge. This shows that the witness of the Messiah has been firmly established among you. You aren't lacking any spiritual gift while you eagerly wait for our Master Jesus the Messiah to be revealed. He will keep you strong until the very end, so that you'll be blameless on the day of our Master Jesus the Messiah. God is faithful — he is the one who called you into this fellowship with his Son, Jesus the Messiah our Master.

Stop the Divisions

Brothers and sisters, I urge you in the name of our Master Jesus the Messiah — please, all of you, get on the same page. Don't let there be divisions among you. Be completely united in the same mind and the same purpose. Some people from Chloe's household have told me that you are arguing with each other. Here's what I mean — some of you are saying, "I follow Paul," others say, "I follow Apollos," or "I follow Peter," or "I follow the Messiah."

Has the Messiah been chopped into pieces? Was Paul executed on a stake for you? Were any of you baptized in the name of Paul? I thank God that I didn't baptize any of you except Crispus and Gaius — so no one can claim they were baptized into my name. I also baptized the household of Stephanas, but I don't remember baptizing anyone else. The Messiah didn't send me to go around baptizing people — he sent me to proclaim the good news. And not with clever or fancy speeches, because that would strip the cross of its real power.

The Power of the Cross

The message of the cross sounds like total nonsense to those who are headed for destruction, but to us who are being saved it is the power of God. As it is written: "I will destroy the wisdom of the wise, and I will set aside the intelligence of the intellectuals." So where does that leave the philosopher? Where is the scholar? Where is the expert debater of this age? God has shown that the wisdom of this world is just plain foolishness.

Since the world with all its wisdom failed to know God, God was pleased to use the so-called foolishness of our message to save those who believe. Jews demand miraculous signs and Greeks look for logic and philosophy — but we proclaim a Messiah who was executed on a cross. To the Jews this is a scandal. To the Greeks it's just plain stupid. But to those who are called — both Jews and Greeks — the Messiah is the power of God and the wisdom of God. Because the foolishness of God is wiser than any human wisdom, and the weakness of God is stronger than any human strength.

God Chose the Unlikely

Just look at yourselves, brothers and sisters. When you were called, not many of you were wise by human standards, not many were powerful, and not many came from important families. Instead God chose the foolish things of the world to embarrass the wise, and he chose the weak things of the world to embarrass the strong. God chose the insignificant things, the despised things — the things the world counts as nothing — to bring to nothing the things the world thinks are everything.

He did this so that no one could ever stand before him and boast. It is because of him that you belong to Christ Jesus — who has become for us the wisdom of God, our righteousness, our separation, and our ransom. So as the Scripture says: "If anyone is going to boast, let them boast about the Master."

1 Corinthians Chapter 2

Paul Preaches Christ Crucified

When I came to you, brothers and sisters, I didn't arrive with impressive words or deep philosophy to tell you about the mystery of God. I made a firm decision that while I was with you I would focus on one thing only — Jesus the Messiah and his death on the cross. I came to you in weakness, feeling timid and honestly trembling with fear. My message and my preaching weren't delivered with clever, persuasive talk designed to sound wise. Instead they came with a demonstration of the Spirit and of power — so that your faith wouldn't rest on human wisdom but on the power of God.

God's Hidden Wisdom

Now, we do speak wisdom among those who are spiritually mature — but it isn't the wisdom of this world or of the leaders of this age, who are all fading away. We speak the hidden wisdom of God — a mystery he planned for our glory before the world even began. None of the rulers of this world understood this wisdom. If they had, they never would have executed the Master of Glory on a cross.

But as the Scripture says:

"No eye has seen, no ear has heard, and no human mind has ever imagined the things God has prepared for those who love him."

The Spirit Reveals What Is Hidden

But God has revealed these things to us through his Spirit. The Spirit searches through everything — even the deepest secrets of God. Think about it — who knows a person's thoughts except their own spirit living inside them? In the same way, no one knows the thoughts of God except the Spirit of God. And we haven't received the spirit of the world — we have received the Spirit who comes from God, so that we can understand the things God has freely given us.

We don't talk about these things using words taught by human wisdom. We use words taught by the Spirit, explaining spiritual truths in spiritual terms. A person who lives only by natural instincts doesn't accept the things that come from the Spirit of God. To them it's all nonsense — they can't understand it because these things can only be understood through the Spirit. But the person who has the Spirit can evaluate everything clearly, while they themselves aren't subject to the judgment of the world. As it is written: "Who has known the mind of the Lord well enough to teach him?"

But we have the mind of the Messiah.

1 Corinthians Chapter 3

You Are Still Not Ready

Brothers and sisters, I couldn't talk to you as spiritual people. I had to speak to you as people still driven by your natural human desires — like infants in the faith. I fed you milk, not solid food, because you weren't ready for it yet. And honestly, you're still not ready. You are still living by your own human nature. As long as there is jealousy and fighting among you, aren't you just acting like people of the world? When one of you says "I follow Paul" and another says "I follow Apollos" — aren't you acting just like everyone else?

We Are Just Servants

Who is Apollos anyway? And who is Paul? We are just servants through whom you came to believe — each of us doing the specific job the Master gave us. I planted the seed, and Apollos watered it, but it was God who made it grow. So the one who plants doesn't matter, and the one who waters doesn't matter either. The only one who matters is God — because he is the one who makes things grow. The one who plants and the one who waters are on the same team, and each will be rewarded based on their own work. We are coworkers with God. You are God's field. You are God's building.

Build on the Right Foundation

Using the grace God gave me, I laid the foundation like a skilled builder, and now others are building on top of it. But everyone needs to be very careful how they build. No one can lay any foundation other than the one already there — which is Jesus the Messiah.

If anyone builds on this foundation using gold, silver, or precious stones — or wood, hay, or straw — their work will eventually be exposed for what it is. The day of judgment will make it clear, because it arrives with fire. That fire will test the quality of each person's work. If what someone built survives the fire, they'll receive a reward. If their work burns up, they'll suffer a great loss. They themselves will still be saved — but just barely, like someone stumbling out through the flames.

You Are God's Temple

Don't you realize that you are God's temple and that the Spirit of God lives in you? If anyone destroys God's temple, God will destroy them — because God's temple is holy. And that is exactly what you are.

Stop Fooling Yourselves

Don't fool yourselves. If any of you think you are wise by the standards of this world, you need to become a fool first — so that you can become truly wise. The wisdom of this world is complete nonsense to God. As it is written: "He catches the wise in their own clever traps," and again, "The Lord knows that the thoughts of the wise are utterly useless."

So stop bragging about following human leaders. Everything already belongs to you — Paul, Apollos, Peter, the world, life, death, the present, the future. All of it belongs to you. You belong to the Messiah. And the Messiah belongs to God.

1 Corinthians Chapter 4

Servants and Stewards

Think of us simply as servants of the Messiah and trusted managers of the hidden truths of God. The most important thing required of a trusted manager is that they be found faithful and reliable. As for me, it matters very little whether I am judged by you or by any human court. I don't even judge myself. My conscience is clear — but that doesn't mean I am innocent. The only one who truly judges me is the Master. So don't jump to conclusions before the right time. Wait until the Master comes. He will bring to light everything currently hidden in darkness and reveal the true intentions of every heart. At that time each person will receive their recognition from God.

Don't Go Beyond What Is Written

Brothers and sisters, I have used myself and Apollos as examples for your benefit — so you could learn from us what it means to not go beyond what is written. That way no one will be arrogant, taking sides with one leader against another. After all, who made you so special? What do you have that you didn't receive as a gift? And if you received it as a gift, why do you brag as if you earned it yourself?

Paul's Hardships as an Apostle

You already think you have everything you need! You think you've already become rich! You've started acting like kings — without us! I actually wish you really were kings, so that we could rule alongside you. But it seems to me that God has put us apostles on display at the very end of the line — like prisoners condemned to die in an arena. We have become a spectacle to the whole world — to angels and humans alike. We are fools for the sake of the Messiah, while you are so wise. We are weak, but you are strong. You are honored, while we are treated with contempt.

Right up to this very moment we go hungry and thirsty, we dress in rags, we are treated roughly, and we have no permanent home. We wear ourselves out working with our own hands. When people insult us, we bless them. When we are persecuted, we endure it. When we are slandered, we respond with kindness. We have become like the world's garbage — the scum of the earth — right up to this very day.

Paul's Fatherly Warning

I'm not writing this to make you feel ashamed. I'm writing to warn you as my own dear children. Even if you had ten thousand teachers in the Messiah, you don't have many fathers. I became your father through the good news — in the Messiah Jesus. So I'm asking you — follow my example.

This is why I sent Timothy to you. He is my dear and faithful son in the Master. He will remind you of my way of life in the Messiah, just as I teach it everywhere in every church.

I Am Coming

Some of you have become arrogant, thinking I'm not actually coming to visit. But I will come very soon, if the Master is willing. And when I get there I won't just listen to the fancy talk of these arrogant people — I want to see whether they have any real power. The kingdom of God isn't about talk — it's about power.

So what do you prefer? Should I come to you with a rod for discipline, or should I come with love and a gentle spirit?

1 Corinthians Chapter 5

Remove the Immoral Man

It is actually being reported everywhere that there is sexual immorality among you — and of a kind that doesn't even happen among the heathens. A man is living with his father's wife. And instead of being brokenhearted about it you are acting proud. Shouldn't the man who did this be removed from your circle?

Even though I am not there with you in person I am there in spirit. And I have already passed judgment on the man who did this just as if I were standing right there. When you gather together in the name of our Master Jesus and I am with you in spirit — with the power of our Master Jesus — you must hand this man over to Satan. The goal is the destruction of his fleshly desires so that his spirit may be saved on the day of the Master.

A Little Leaven Spreads Through Everything

Your bragging is not good at all. Don't you know that a little leaven spreads through the whole batch of dough? Clean out the old leaven so that you can be a fresh new batch — which is what you really are, people without leaven. For the Messiah our Passover Lamb has been sacrificed for us. So let us keep the feast — not with the old leaven of malice and wickedness but with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth.

Do Not Associate with the Immoral

I wrote to you in my previous letter not to associate with people who are sexually immoral. I didn't mean the people of this world who are immoral or greedy or swindlers or idol worshipers — to avoid them you would literally have to leave the planet.

What I meant was this — do not associate with anyone who calls themselves a brother or sister but is sexually immoral, or greedy, or an idol worshiper, or a slanderer, or a drunkard, or a swindler. Don't even sit down to eat a meal with someone like that.

Judge Those Inside the Church

Is it my job to judge those outside the community? Of course not. But aren't you responsible for judging those inside? God will judge those on the outside. As for you —

Remove the wicked person from among yourselves.

1 Corinthians Chapter 6

Don't Take Each Other to Court

How dare any of you, when you have a legal dispute with another believer, take it to a secular court to be judged by people who don't follow God's ways? Why aren't you taking it to God's people instead? Don't you realize that God's people will one day judge the whole world? If you are going to judge the world, surely you can handle these small disputes. Don't you know that we will even judge angels? If that's true, we can certainly handle the everyday matters of this life.

So why do you appoint judges who have no standing in the faith community to settle your disputes? I say this to shame you. Is there really not a single wise person among you who can settle a disagreement between brothers? Instead one brother sues another — right in front of unbelievers!

The very fact that you are suing each other means you have already lost. Why not just accept the wrong done to you? Why not just let yourselves be cheated? Instead you are the ones doing wrong and cheating — and doing it to your own brothers and sisters!

Don't Be Fooled

Don't you know that people who do unrighteous things will not inherit the kingdom of God? Don't be fooled. People who are sexually immoral, idol worshipers, adulterers, those who practice homosexuality, thieves, the greedy, drunkards, slanderers, or swindlers — none of these will inherit the kingdom of God. And some of you used to be exactly like that! But you were washed clean, you were set apart, and you were made right with God — in the name of the Master Jesus the Messiah and by the Spirit of our God.

Your Body Belongs to God

You say, "I am allowed to do anything." Maybe so — but not everything is good for you. And even if I am allowed to do anything, I will not let anything become my master. You say, "Food is for the stomach and the stomach for food." True — but God will do away with both in the end. The body was not made for sexual immorality. It was made for the Master, and the Master cares about the body. Just as God raised the Master from the dead by his power, he will also raise us up.

Don't you realize that your bodies are actually parts of the Messiah's own body? Should I take a part of the Messiah and join it to a prostitute? Absolutely not! Don't you know that a man who joins himself to a prostitute becomes one body with her? As the Scripture says, "The two will become one flesh." But the person who is joined to the Master is one spirit with him.

Run from Sexual Immorality

Run away from sexual immorality! Every other sin a person commits happens outside the body — but the person who is sexually immoral sins against their own body. Don't you know that your body is a temple for the Holy Spirit who lives in you — the Spirit given to you by God? You don't belong to yourselves. You were bought at a great price. So use your body to bring glory to God.

1 Corinthians Chapter 7

Marriage and Self-Control

Now, regarding the questions you wrote to me about — it is actually a good thing for a man not to have sexual relations with a woman. But because there is so much sexual immorality in the world, each man should have his own wife and each woman should have her own husband. A husband should fulfill his marital duty to his wife, and a wife should do the same for her husband. The wife doesn't have sole authority over her own body — her husband shares it. In the same way, the husband doesn't have sole authority over his own body — his wife shares it.

Don't deprive each other of intimacy unless you both agree to it for a short time to focus on prayer. But then come back together again so that Satan doesn't tempt you because of your lack of self-control. I'm saying this as a concession, not a command. I wish everyone could be single like I am — but each person has their own gift from God, one kind for one person and a different kind for another.

To the Unmarried and Widows

To the unmarried and to the widows I say — it is good to stay single as I am. But if you can't control yourself, get married. It is far better to marry than to burn with passion.

To the Married

To those who are already married I have a command — and this comes from the Master, not just from me. A wife must not leave her husband. But if she does leave, she should either remain unmarried or be reconciled to her husband. And a husband must not divorce his wife.

When One Spouse Is an Unbeliever

To the rest of you I have this to say — this is my best judgment, though not a direct command from the Master. If a brother has a wife who is not a believer and she is happy to live with him, he should not divorce her. And if a woman has a husband who is not a believer and he is happy to live with her, she should not divorce him. The unbelieving husband is brought closer to God through his believing wife, and the unbelieving wife is brought closer through her believing husband. Otherwise your children would have no connection to God at all — but as it is, they are covered.

But if the unbelieving partner chooses to leave, let them go. In that case the brother or sister is not bound. God has called us to live in peace. And wife — how do you know whether you will bring your husband to God? Or husband — how do you know whether you will bring your wife to God?

Stay as You Are

Generally speaking, each person should live in the situation the Master assigned to them when God called them. This is the rule I give in all the churches. Were you already circumcised when you were called? Don't try to hide it. Were you uncircumcised? Don't get circumcised. Circumcision or the lack of it doesn't matter — what matters is keeping God's commandments. Stay as you were when God called you.

Were you a slave when you were called? Don't let it bother you — though if you have the chance to become free, take it. A slave who is called by the Master is the Master's freed person. And a free person who is called belongs to the Messiah. You were bought at a great price — don't become slaves of men. Brothers and sisters, stay close to God in whatever state you were in when he called you.

About Those Who Have Never Married

Now about those who have never been married — I don't have a direct command from the Master on this, but I'll give you my honest opinion as someone who has received God's mercy. Because of the difficulties of the times we're living in, I think it's best to stay as you are. Are you married? Don't seek a divorce. Are you single? Don't look for a wife. But if you do get married you haven't sinned. And if a young woman gets married she hasn't sinned either. Those who marry will face the added pressures of daily life together — and I'm trying to spare you from that.

Time Is Running Out

What I mean, brothers and sisters, is this — time is short. From now on, those with wives should live with such focus that it's as if they had none. Those who mourn should not be consumed by grief. Those who are happy should not be distracted by joy. Those who buy things should not act as if they own them. And those who use the things of this world should not get wrapped up in them. This world in its present form is passing away.

Undivided Devotion

I want you to be free from worry. An unmarried man is focused on the Master's business — on how he can please the Master. But a married man is focused on the things of this world — on how he can please his wife — and his attention is divided. An unmarried woman or a young woman who has never married is focused on the Master's business, devoted in body and spirit. But a married woman is focused on the things of this world — on how she can please her husband. I'm saying this for your own good, not to restrict you. I want you to live in a way that is honorable and allows you to serve the Master without distraction.

If a man feels he is not being fair to the woman he is engaged to — if his passions are strong and he feels they should marry — let him do what he wants. It's not a sin. They should get married. But if a man has the self-control to stay single and has made up his mind to do so, he does a good thing too. The one who marries does well — but the one who stays single does even better.

A Final Word About Widows

A wife is bound to her husband as long as he lives. But if her husband dies she is free to marry whoever she wants — as long as he belongs to the Master. In my opinion though she will be happier if she stays as she is. And I believe I have the Spirit of God in saying this.

1 Corinthians Chapter 8

Knowledge vs. Love

Now, regarding food that has been sacrificed to idols — we all know the saying, "We all have knowledge." But remember, knowledge tends to make people arrogant, while love is what actually builds people up. If anyone thinks they really know something, they haven't yet reached the kind of understanding they ought to have. But if someone truly loves God, that person is known and recognized by him.

There Is Only One God

So about eating meat that was sacrificed to idols — we know that an idol in this world isn't actually anything real, and we know there is no God but one. Even if there are so-called gods in heaven or on earth — and indeed many people believe in many gods and many lords — for us there is only one God: the Father. Everything comes from him and we live for him. And there is only one Master: Jesus the Messiah. Everything exists through him and we have our life through him.

Don't Wound a Weak Conscience

But here's the problem — not everyone has this understanding. Some people are so used to idols that even now, when they eat such meat, they think of it as a sacrifice to a real god. Because their conscience isn't fully settled in the truth, they feel guilty eating it. Now, food itself doesn't bring us closer to God. We aren't worse off if we don't eat and we aren't better off if we do.

But be very careful. Don't let your freedom to eat become a stumbling block for someone whose faith is still fragile. Think about it — if someone with a sensitive conscience sees you, the one who knows better, sitting down to eat in an idol's temple, won't they be encouraged to eat that meat even though they feel it's wrong? Your knowledge ends up destroying a brother or sister for whom the Messiah died.

When you wound the conscience of a fellow believer like that, you are sinning against the Messiah himself. So if what I eat is going to cause my brother or sister to stumble, I will never eat meat again — I refuse to be the reason they fall.

1 Corinthians Chapter 9

Paul Defends His Apostleship

Am I not free? Am I not an apostle? Haven't I seen Jesus our Master with my own eyes? And aren't you Corinthians the living proof of my work for him? Even if others don't accept me as an apostle, you certainly should — because you are the living seal that proves my mission. You exist because I came to you.

The Right to Be Supported

This is my defense to those who want to put me on trial. Don't we have the right to food and drink? Don't we have the right to bring a believing wife along with us, just like the other apostles, the Master's brothers, and Peter? Or is it only Barnabas and I who are required to work a day job to support ourselves?

Think about it — does a soldier go to war at his own expense? Who plants a vineyard and doesn't eat its grapes? Who shepherds a flock and doesn't drink some of the milk? I'm not just making a human argument here — the law says the same thing. In the law of Moses it is written: "Do not muzzle an ox while it is treading out the grain." Do you think God was only worried about oxen? Wasn't he really talking about us? Yes, this was written for our sake. The one who plows and the one who threshes should both expect to share in the harvest.

If we have sown spiritual seeds among you, is it too much to expect some material support in return? If others have this right over you, shouldn't we have it even more? But we haven't used this right. Instead we put up with everything so we don't create any obstacle for the good news of the Messiah. Don't you know that those who work in the temple get their food from the temple, and those who serve at the altar get a share of the offerings? In the same way the Master commanded that those who preach the good news should make their living from it.

Paul Preaches Free of Charge

But I haven't used any of these rights — and I'm not writing this now to ask for anything. Honestly I would rather die than have anyone take away what I take pride in. Preaching the good news isn't something I boast about — it's something I have to do. Woe to me if I don't preach it! If I did this by my own choice I could expect a reward. But I didn't choose this — it was entrusted to me. So what is my reward? Simply this — that in my preaching I can offer the good news completely free of charge, without making full use of my rights as a preacher.

All Things to All People

Even though I am a free man and answer to no one, I have made myself a servant to everyone in order to win as many as possible. To the Jews I became like a Jew to win the Jews. To those under the law I became like one under the law — though I am not personally bound by it — to win those under the law. To those outside the law I became like one outside the law — though I am not without God's law, but bound by the law of the Messiah — to win those outside the law. To the weak I became weak to win the weak. I have become all things to all people so that by every possible means I might save some. I do all this for the sake of the good news so that I can share in its blessings.

Run to Win

Don't you know that in a race all the runners run, but only one gets the prize? Run in such a way that you win. Everyone who competes trains with strict discipline — they do it for a prize that fades away, but we do it for a prize that lasts forever. So I don't run without a clear goal. I don't box like someone just swinging at the air. No — I discipline my body and keep it under control so that after preaching to others I myself won't be disqualified.

1 Corinthians Chapter 10

Lessons from Israel's History

Brothers and sisters, I don't want you to forget what happened to our ancestors. They were all under the cloud of God's protection and they all walked through the sea on dry ground. In a sense they were all baptized into Moses' leadership through that cloud and that sea. They all ate the same spiritual food and drank the same spiritual drink — they were drinking from a spiritual Rock that followed them, and that Rock was the Messiah.

But even with all those blessings God was not pleased with most of them, and their bodies ended up scattered across the wilderness. These things happened as a warning for us — so that we wouldn't crave evil things the way they did.

Don't become idol worshipers like some of them were. As the Scripture says, "The people sat down to eat and drink and got up to indulge in pagan revelry." Don't commit sexual immorality as some of them did — twenty-three thousand of them died in a single day because of it. Don't test the Messiah as some of them did, only to be destroyed by snakes. And don't grumble and complain as some of them did, which led to their destruction.

All these things happened to them as examples — written down to warn us, the people living at the very end of the ages. So if you think you are standing firm, be careful that you don't fall. No temptation has come your way that isn't common to everyone. But God is faithful — he will never let you be tempted beyond what you can handle. When you are tempted he will always provide a way out so that you can endure it.

Run from Idolatry

So my dear friends — run away from idolatry. I'm speaking to you as sensible people, so think about what I'm saying. When we bless the cup of blessing, isn't that a shared participation in the blood of the Messiah? When we break the bread, isn't that a shared participation in the body of the Messiah? Because there is one loaf of bread, we — though many — are one body, for we all share that one loaf.

Look at the people of Israel — don't those who eat the sacrifices participate in what happens at the altar? So what am I saying — that an idol is something real, or that meat sacrificed to an idol is special? No. I'm saying that what pagans sacrifice, they sacrifice to demons, not to God. And I don't want you anywhere near that. You cannot drink the cup of the Master and the cup of demons at the same time. You cannot sit at the Master's table and the table of demons. Are we trying to make the Master jealous? Do we really think we are stronger than he is?

Freedom and the Good of Others

You say, "I am allowed to do anything." Maybe so — but not everything is good for you. You say, "I am allowed to do anything" — but not everything builds people up. Stop looking out only for your own interests. Look out for the interests of others.

Here is the practical rule: eat anything sold in the meat market without asking questions — because "the earth belongs to the Master and everything in it." If an unbeliever invites you to dinner and you want to go, eat whatever is put in front of you without asking questions. But if someone says to you, "This meat was offered to an idol," then don't eat it — not for your own sake but for the sake of the person who told you. I'm talking about their conscience, not yours. Why should my freedom be judged by someone else's conscience? If I give thanks to God for my food, why should I be criticized for eating it?

Do Everything for God's Glory

So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do — do it all for the glory of God. Don't cause anyone to stumble — not Jews, not Gentiles, and not the church of God. Follow my example. I try to please everyone in every way — not looking for what is best for me, but for what is best for the many, so that they may be saved.

1 Corinthians Chapter 11

Follow My Example

Follow my example, just as I follow the example of the Messiah.

I want to commend you for remembering me in everything and for holding to the traditions just as I passed them on to you. But I want you to understand something important about how things are ordered — the head of every man is the Messiah, the head of a woman is the man, and the head of the Messiah is God.

Head Coverings

Every man who prays or prophesies with his head covered brings shame on the Messiah. But every woman who prays or prophesies with her head uncovered brings shame on her husband — it is the same as if her head were shaved. If a woman won't cover her head, she might as well cut all her hair off. But if it's a disgrace for a woman to have her hair cut short or her head shaved, she should keep it covered.

A man shouldn't cover his head because he is the image and glory of God — but the woman is the glory of the man. Man didn't come from woman, but woman from man. And man wasn't created for the sake of the woman, but woman for the sake of the man. Because of this, and because the angels are watching, a woman should wear a symbol of authority on her head.

Even so, in the Master, woman is not independent of man and man is not independent of woman. Just as woman originally came from man, now every man comes into the world through a woman — and everything ultimately comes from God. Judge for yourselves — is it proper for a woman to pray to God in public with her head uncovered? Doesn't nature itself teach you that if a man has long hair it is a disgrace, but if a woman has long hair it is her glory? Her hair is given to her as a natural covering. If anyone wants to argue about this, just know that we have no other custom, and neither do any of the other churches of God.

Divisions at the Lord's Supper

Now, regarding what I'm about to say — I cannot praise you. When you meet together it seems to do more harm than good. I hear that when you gather as a community there are cliques and divisions among you — and I believe it. I suppose there have to be factions among you so that those who are genuine can be clearly seen.

When you get together you aren't really eating the Master's Supper at all. Each of you rushes ahead to eat your own meal first — one person goes hungry while another gets drunk. Don't you have homes to eat and drink in? Or do you despise the church of God and want to humiliate those who have nothing? What can I say? Should I praise you for this? Absolutely not.

The Lord's Supper

This is what I received from the Master and passed on to you — the Master Jesus, on the night he was betrayed, took bread. After giving thanks he broke it and said, "This is my body, which is for you. Do this in remembrance of me." In the same way, after supper he took the cup and said, "This cup is the new covenant in my blood. Do this, every time you drink it, in remembrance of me."

Every time you eat this bread and drink this cup you are announcing the Master's death until he comes back. So whoever eats the bread or drinks the cup of the Master in an unworthy way is guilty of sinning against the body and blood of the Master.

Examine Yourself First

A person should examine themselves before eating the bread and drinking from the cup. Anyone who eats and drinks without recognizing what the body means is eating and drinking judgment upon themselves. This is why many of you are weak and sick, and quite a few have even died.

If we would honestly evaluate ourselves we wouldn't fall under judgment. But when the Master judges us he is disciplining us so that we won't be condemned along with the rest of the world.

So then brothers and sisters — when you come together to eat, wait for one another. If someone is starving they should eat at home first so that your meeting doesn't result in judgment. I will sort out the rest of the details when I arrive.

1 Corinthians Chapter 12

Don't Be Confused About Spiritual Gifts

Brothers and sisters, I don't want you to be confused about spiritual gifts. You remember that back when you were pagans you were led astray by silent idols, following wherever you were pushed. So I want to make this clear — no one speaking by the Spirit of God ever says "Jesus is cursed," and no one can truly say "Jesus is Master" except by the Holy Spirit.

One Spirit, Many Gifts

There are different kinds of spiritual gifts, but they all come from the same Spirit. There are different ways to serve, but we all serve the same Master. There are different ways that God works, but it is the same God who powers everything in everyone.

The Spirit shows up in each person for the good of the whole group. To one person the Spirit gives a message of wisdom. To another, a message of knowledge from that same Spirit. To someone else, special faith. To another, gifts of healing. One person receives the power to work miracles, another to prophesy, another to tell the difference between spirits, another to speak in different languages, and another to translate those languages. But the one and only Spirit is behind all of these — giving them to each person exactly as he chooses.

One Body, Many Parts

Think of it this way — the human body is one unit but it has many parts. Even though there are many parts they all make up one body. It's the same with the Messiah. We were all baptized by one Spirit into one body — whether Jews or Gentiles, slaves or free — and we were all given that one Spirit to drink.

The body isn't just one part — it's many. If the foot says "I'm not a hand so I don't belong to the body," that doesn't make it any less a part of the body. If the ear says "I'm not an eye so I don't belong to the body," it's still part of the body. If the whole body were an eye, how would you hear? If the whole body were an ear, how would you smell?

But God has placed each part in the body exactly where he wanted it. If everything were just one part, where would the body be? As it is, there are many parts but only one body.

The eye can't say to the hand "I don't need you." And the head can't say to the feet "I don't need you." In fact the parts that seem weakest are actually the most necessary. The parts we think are less honorable we treat with extra care. We give special attention to the parts we'd rather not show, while the parts we're proud of don't need that. God put the body together so that more honor goes to the parts that had less — so there would be no division in the body, but every part would have the same concern for the others. If one part suffers, every part suffers. If one part is honored, every part celebrates.

You Are the Body of the Messiah

You are the body of the Messiah — and each one of you is a specific part of it. In the church God has appointed first apostles, second prophets, third teachers, then those who work miracles, then those with gifts of healing, those who help others, those who lead and organize, and those who speak in different languages.

Is everyone an apostle? No. Is everyone a prophet? No. Is everyone a teacher? No. Does everyone work miracles or have gifts of healing? No. Does everyone speak in languages or translate them? Of course not.

You should value the greater gifts deeply. And now I'm going to show you a way that is better than all of them.

1 Corinthians Chapter 13

The Greatest Gift

Even if I could speak every human language and the languages of angels, but don't have love — I am nothing more than a noisy gong or a clanging cymbal. Even if I have the gift of prophecy and understand every deep mystery and possess all knowledge — and even if I have enough faith to move mountains — but don't have love, I am nothing at all. Even if I give away everything I own to feed the poor, and even if I hand over my body to be burned — but don't have love, it means nothing.

What Love Looks Like

Love is patient. Love is kind. Love isn't jealous. It doesn't brag and it isn't arrogant. It isn't rude. It doesn't push for its own way. It isn't quick-tempered and it doesn't keep a record of wrongs. Love doesn't celebrate when things are unfair — but it celebrates whenever truth wins. Love protects everything, trusts everything, hopes for everything, and endures everything.

Love Never Fails

Love never fails. Prophecies will come to an end. Tongues will stop. Knowledge will be surpassed. Right now we only know in part and we only prophesy in part. But when what is perfect arrives, these partial things will be set aside.

When I was a child I talked like a child, thought like a child, and reasoned like a child. But when I became a man I put those childish ways behind me. Right now we're looking into a dim mirror, seeing only a blurry reflection. But then we will see face to face. Right now my knowledge is partial — but then I will know fully, just as I am fully known by God.

So three things remain — faith, hope, and love. But the greatest of these is love.

1 Corinthians Chapter 14

Make Love Your Goal

Make love your primary goal. At the same time, truly desire spiritual gifts — especially the ability to prophesy, to speak God's truth clearly. Someone who speaks in a tongue isn't speaking to people but to God. No one understands them — they are speaking deep mysteries in the Spirit. But the person who prophesies speaks to people to build them up, encourage them, and comfort them.

The person speaking in a tongue builds up themselves, but the person who prophesies builds up the entire church. I'd be happy if all of you spoke in tongues — but I'd much rather you prophesy. The one who prophesies is more useful than the one who speaks in tongues, unless someone translates so the whole church can learn something.

Make Yourself Understood

Think about it, brothers and sisters — if I come to you speaking in tongues, how does that help you? It only helps if I bring you a revelation, some knowledge, a prophecy, or a clear teaching. Even with instruments like a flute or a harp — if the notes aren't clear, how will anyone recognize the tune? If a bugle gives a confused signal, who is going to prepare for battle? It's the same with you. If you don't speak clear, understandable words, you're just talking into the air.

There are all kinds of languages in the world and none of them are without meaning. But if I don't understand the language being spoken, I'm a foreigner to the speaker and the speaker is a foreigner to me. So since you are so eager for spiritual gifts, focus on the ones that build up the church.

Pray with Your Mind Too

Anyone who speaks in a tongue should pray for the ability to interpret it. If I pray in a tongue, my spirit is praying — but my mind is completely uninvolved. So what should I do? I'll pray with my spirit and I'll also pray with my mind. I'll sing with my spirit and I'll also sing with my mind. Otherwise, if you give a blessing in the spirit, how can someone else say "Amen" to your prayer of thanks? They have no idea what you're saying. You might be giving a wonderful prayer, but the other person isn't being built up at all.

I thank God that I speak in tongues more than any of you. But in a church gathering I would rather speak five understandable words to teach others than ten thousand words in a tongue.

Be Mature in Your Thinking

Brothers and sisters, don't be childish in your thinking. Be innocent when it comes to evil — but be mature in how you think. It is written in the law: "By people of strange tongues and by the lips of foreigners I will speak to this people — and even then they will not listen to me, says the Lord." So tongues are a sign for unbelievers, not for believers. But prophecy is for believers, not for unbelievers.

Imagine the whole church meets together and everyone starts speaking in tongues at once. If some ordinary people or unbelievers walk in, won't they say you've all lost your minds? But if everyone is prophesying and an unbeliever walks in, they are convicted by what they hear. The secrets of their heart are laid bare and they will fall on their face and worship God, declaring, "God is truly among you!"

Order in the Church

So what's the bottom line? When you meet together, everyone has something to contribute — a psalm, a teaching, a revelation, a tongue, or an interpretation. Everything must be done to build each other up. If anyone speaks in a tongue, limit it to two or three at most, one at a time, and someone must interpret. If there's no interpreter, they should stay quiet and speak only to themselves and to God.

Let two or three prophets speak and let the others weigh what is said. If a revelation comes to someone else while the first person is speaking, the first speaker should stop and give way. You can all take turns prophesying so that everyone learns and everyone is encouraged. The spirit of a prophet is under the prophet's own control — God is not a God of disorder but of peace, as is the rule in all the churches.

Women in the Assembly

Women should keep quiet during the church gathering. They are not permitted to speak out of turn but should show respect for the order of things, as the law also indicates. If they want to learn something specific, they should ask their own husbands at home — it is disruptive for a woman to interrupt the gathering.

A Final Word

Did the word of God start with you? Or are you the only people it has reached? If anyone thinks they are a prophet or spiritually gifted, they should recognize that what I am writing is a command from the Master. If anyone ignores this, they themselves will be ignored.

So then brothers and sisters — be eager to prophesy, and don't forbid speaking in tongues. Just make sure everything is done in a proper and orderly way.

1 Corinthians Chapter 15

The Heart of the Good News

Brothers and sisters, I want to remind you of the good news I preached to you. You received it, you've built your lives on it, and it is what saves you — provided you hold firmly to the message I gave you. Otherwise your faith was just for show.

Here is the core of what I passed on to you, which was passed on to me:

The Messiah died for our sins, exactly as the Scriptures predicted. He was buried. He was raised to life on the third day, exactly as the Scriptures predicted. And he was seen.

He appeared to Peter, then to the Twelve. After that he appeared to more than five hundred brothers and sisters at the same time — most of whom are still alive today, though some have died. Then he appeared to James, then to all the apostles. Last of all, as if to someone born at the wrong time, he appeared to me.

I am the least of the apostles. I don't even deserve the title because I used to persecute the church of God. But by the grace of God I am what I am. His grace toward me wasn't wasted — I have worked harder than all of them, though it wasn't really me but the grace of God working through me. Regardless of who preached it, this is what we proclaim and this is what you believed.

If There Is No Resurrection

Now if we preach that the Messiah was raised from the dead, how can some of you say there is no resurrection of the dead? If there's no resurrection then the Messiah wasn't raised either. And if the Messiah wasn't raised, our preaching is useless and your faith is empty. More than that — we'd be liars about God, because we testified that he raised the Messiah, which he wouldn't have done if the dead aren't raised.

If the dead aren't raised then your faith is a delusion and you are still trapped in your sins. It would also mean that everyone who died believing in the Messiah is simply gone. If our hope in the Messiah is only for this life, we are the most pitiful people on earth.

But the Messiah Has Been Raised

But the truth is — the Messiah has been raised from the dead. He is the first of the harvest — the guarantee that those who have died will follow. Since death came through one man, the resurrection of the dead also comes through one man. Just as everyone dies because of Adam, everyone will be made alive because of the Messiah. But there is an order — the Messiah first, then those who belong to him when he returns. Then comes the end, when he hands the kingdom over to God the Father, after he has done away with every ruler, authority, and power. He must reign until he puts all his enemies under his feet. The very last enemy to be destroyed is death.

Now if there is no resurrection, why do people get baptized on behalf of the dead? Why do we constantly put our lives in danger? I face death every single day — that's as true as the pride I have in you through the Messiah Jesus our Master. If I fought wild beasts in Ephesus for purely human reasons, what did I gain? If the dead aren't raised, we might as well say — let's eat and drink, for tomorrow we die.

Don't be fooled — bad company corrupts good character. Wake up and stop sinning. Some of you don't even know God — I say this to your shame.

What Kind of Body Will We Have?

Someone will ask — how are the dead raised? What kind of body will they have? What a foolish question. When you plant a seed it doesn't grow unless it first dies. What you plant is just a bare seed — maybe wheat or something else — not the full plant that will eventually come up. God gives it the body he chose, and every seed gets its own body.

Not all flesh is the same — humans have one kind, animals another, birds another, fish another. There are heavenly bodies and earthly bodies. The beauty of the heavenly ones is one thing and the beauty of the earthly ones is another. The sun has its own glory, the moon another, the stars another — and even stars differ from each other in brightness.

It's the same with the resurrection of the dead.

Buried in decay — raised in immortality. Buried in shame — raised in glory. Buried in weakness — raised in power. Buried a natural body — raised a spiritual body.

The Scripture says: "The first man, Adam, became a living soul." The last Adam — the Messiah — became a life-giving spirit. The spiritual didn't come first — the natural came first, then the spiritual. The first man was made of dust from the earth. The second man is from heaven. Those made of dust are like the man of dust. Those who are heavenly are like the Heavenly Man. Just as we have carried the image of the man of dust, we will also carry the image of the Heavenly Man.

We Will All Be Changed

Listen carefully, brothers and sisters — flesh and blood cannot inherit the kingdom of God. What decays cannot inherit what lasts forever. I'm telling you a mystery — we won't all die, but we will all be changed. It will happen in an instant, in the blink of an eye, at the sound of the last trumpet. The trumpet will blast, the dead will be raised immortal, and we will be transformed. This body that decays must be clothed with a body that never will.

When that happens the prophecy will come true:

"Death has been swallowed up in victory!"

"O Death, where is your victory? O Death, where is your sting?"

The sting of death is sin, and the power of sin is the law. But thank God — he gives us the victory through our Master, Jesus the Messiah.

Stand Firm

So then, my dear brothers and sisters — stand firm. Let nothing move you. Always give yourselves fully to the work of the Master, because you know that your hard work for him is never a waste.

1 Corinthians Chapter 16

The Collection for God's People

Now regarding the collection for God's people — follow the same instructions I gave to the churches in Galatia. On the first day of every week each of you should set aside a portion of your income, saving it up according to how much you've been blessed. Do this so that no rushed collections have to be made when I arrive. When I get there I will send the people you approve — with letters of introduction — to take your gift to Jerusalem. If it seems right for me to go too, they can travel with me.

Paul's Travel Plans

I plan to visit you after I pass through Macedonia. I might stay with you for a while or even spend the winter, so that you can help send me on my way to wherever I go next. I don't want to just see you in passing — I hope to spend some real time with you, if the Master permits. I'll be staying in Ephesus until Pentecost, because a great door for ministry has opened here, even though there is strong opposition.

Timothy and Apollos

If Timothy comes, make sure he feels welcome among you — he is doing the Master's work just as I am. Don't let anyone look down on him. Send him on his way in peace so he can return to me, as I am expecting him along with the other brothers.

As for our brother Apollos, I strongly encouraged him to visit you with the other brothers. He wasn't ready to come just yet, but he will visit when the time is right.

Final Encouragements

Stay alert. Stand firm in the faith. Be brave. Be strong. Let everything you do be done in love.

You know the household of Stephanas — they were the first believers in Achaia and have dedicated themselves to serving God's people. I urge you brothers and sisters to follow the lead of people like them and everyone who joins in the hard work of ministry. I was overjoyed when Stephanas, Fortunatus, and Achaicus arrived — they made up for your absence and gave my spirit a real lift. Make sure you recognize people like that for what they are worth.

Final Greetings

The churches in Asia send their greetings. Aquila and Prisca send you warm greetings in the Master, along with the church that meets in their home. All the brothers and sisters here send their greetings. Greet one another with a holy kiss.

I, Paul, am writing this greeting with my own hand.

If anyone does not love the Master, let them be cut off. Come, Master Jesus — come quickly!

May the grace of the Master Jesus be with you. My love to all of you in Christ Jesus.
