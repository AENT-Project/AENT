2 Timothy

2 Timothy — (AENT) Accessible English New Testament

This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.

A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."

This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.

Grace & Peace.

2 Timothy Chapter 1

Opening Greeting

Paul, an apostle of Jesus the Messiah by the will of God, according to the promise of life that is in Jesus the Messiah — to Timothy, my beloved son. Grace, mercy, and peace to you from God the Father and Jesus the Messiah our Master.

Fan the Flame

I thank God — whom I serve with a clear conscience as my ancestors did — when I remember you constantly in my prayers night and day. I long to see you and I remember your tears — and I know that seeing you would fill me with joy. I am reminded of your sincere faith which first lived in your grandmother Lois and your mother Eunice — and I am convinced it lives in you as well.

For this reason I remind you to fan into flame the gift of God that is in you through the laying on of my hands. For God did not give us a spirit of fear — but of power, love, and a sound mind.

Don't Be Ashamed

So don't be ashamed of the testimony of our Master or of me his prisoner. Instead join with me in suffering for the good news by the power of God. He saved us and called us to a holy life — not because of anything we did but because of his own purpose and grace. This grace was given to us in Jesus the Messiah before time began but has now been revealed through the appearing of our Savior Jesus the Messiah. He has broken the power of death and brought life and immortality to light through the good news.

I was appointed a herald, an apostle, and a teacher of this message. That is why I am suffering as I am. But I am not ashamed — because I know whom I have believed and I am convinced that he is able to guard what I have entrusted to him until that day.

Hold On and Guard It

Hold on to the pattern of sound teaching you heard from me — in the faith and love that are in Jesus the Messiah. Guard the good trust committed to you through the Holy Spirit who lives in us.

Faithful and Unfaithful Friends

You know that everyone in the province of Asia has deserted me — including Phygelus and Hermogenes.

May the Master show mercy to the household of Onesiphorus — because he often refreshed me and was not ashamed of my chains. When he was in Rome he searched hard for me until he found me. May the Master grant that he will find mercy from God on that day. You know very well how much he helped me in Ephesus.

2 Timothy Chapter 2

Be a Good Soldier

So you my son — be strong in the grace that is in Jesus the Messiah. The things you heard from me in the presence of many witnesses — entrust these to faithful people who will be capable of teaching others as well.

Share in suffering like a good soldier of Jesus the Messiah. No soldier on active duty gets tangled up in the everyday business of life — he focuses on pleasing the one who enlisted him. And if anyone competes as an athlete he doesn't win the prize unless he competes by the rules. The hardworking farmer should be the first to receive a share of the crops. Think about what I am saying — the Master will give you understanding in everything.

The Word of God Is Not Chained

Remember Jesus the Messiah — raised from the dead, descended from David. This is my good news, for which I am suffering to the point of being chained like a criminal. But the word of God is not chained. So I endure everything for the sake of God's chosen people so that they too may obtain the salvation that is in Jesus the Messiah with eternal glory.

Here is a trustworthy saying —

If we died with him we will also live with him. If we endure we will also reign with him. If we deny him he will also deny us. If we are unfaithful he remains faithful — for he cannot deny himself.

Handle the Word Correctly

Keep reminding people of these things. Warn them before God not to fight about words — it is useless and only destroys those who listen. Do your best to present yourself to God as one approved — a worker who has no need to be ashamed, who correctly handles the word of truth.

Avoid godless chatter because those who indulge in it will only become more ungodly. Their teaching spreads like gangrene. Among them are Hymenaeus and Philetus who have wandered away from the truth — claiming that the resurrection has already happened and upsetting the faith of some.

But God's solid foundation stands firm sealed with this inscription — the Master knows those who are his, and everyone who confesses the name of the Master must turn away from wickedness.

Be a Vessel for Honor

In a large house there are not only gold and silver bowls but also ones made of wood and clay — some for special use and some for ordinary use. If anyone cleanses himself from these things he will be a vessel for honor, set apart, useful to the Master and prepared for every good work.

Run from youthful lusts. Instead pursue righteousness, faith, love, and peace — along with those who call on the Master out of a pure heart. Have nothing to do with foolish and ignorant arguments because you know they only start fights. A servant of the Master must not be quarrelsome but kind to everyone, able to teach, and patient when wronged. Gently instruct those who oppose you — in the hope that God will grant them repentance leading to a knowledge of the truth, and that they will come to their senses and escape the trap of the devil, who has taken them captive to do his will.

2 Timothy Chapter 3

Difficult Times Are Coming

Understand this — in the last days very difficult times will come. People will love only themselves and money. They will be boastful, proud, and abusive. Disobedient to their parents, ungrateful, and unholy. They will have no natural affection and will be unforgiving. They will slander others and have no self-control. They will be cruel and hate what is good. They will be traitors — reckless and puffed up with pride — loving pleasure more than they love God.

They will look religious but they will reject the power that makes a person truly godly. Have nothing to do with people like that.

These are the kind of people who worm their way into homes and gain control over vulnerable people burdened by sins and led astray by all kinds of desires — always trying to learn but never able to come to the truth. Just as Jannes and Jambres opposed Moses these people also oppose the truth. Their minds are corrupt and their faith is counterfeit. But they won't get very far — just like those men in the time of Moses their foolishness will eventually be plain for everyone to see.

Follow My Example

But you Timothy have closely followed my teaching, my way of life, my purpose, my faith, my patience, my love, and my endurance. You know about the persecutions and sufferings I went through in Antioch, Iconium, and Lystra — and you know that the Master rescued me from all of them.

In fact everyone who wants to live a godly life in Jesus the Messiah will be persecuted. Meanwhile evil people and impostors will go from bad to worse — deceiving others and being deceived themselves.

The Authority of Scripture

But as for you — continue in what you have learned and have become convinced of, because you know those who taught you. From the time you were a child you have known the Holy Scriptures which are able to give you the wisdom you need for salvation through faith in Jesus the Messiah.

All Scripture is breathed out by God and is useful for teaching, for showing us what is wrong, for correcting faults, and for training us to live righteously. Its purpose is to make the person of God complete — fully equipped for every good work.

2 Timothy Chapter 4

Preach the Word

I solemnly charge you before God and the Master Jesus the Messiah — who will judge the living and the dead when he appears to establish his kingdom. Preach the word. Be ready at all times whether it is convenient or not. Correct, rebuke, and encourage with great patience and careful teaching.

The time is coming when people will no longer listen to sound teaching. Instead they will surround themselves with teachers who tell them exactly what their itching ears want to hear. They will turn their backs on the truth and wander off into myths and fables.

But you — keep a clear head in every situation. Don't be afraid to suffer. Do the work of an evangelist and carry out your ministry to the fullest.

Paul's Final Words

As for me — my life is already being poured out as an offering and the time for my departure is very near. I have fought the good fight. I have finished the race. I have kept the faith. Now the crown of righteousness is waiting for me — the Master, the righteous Judge, will give it to me on that day. And not just to me but to everyone who has eagerly looked forward to his coming.

Come to Me Soon

Do your best to come to me quickly. Demas has abandoned me — he loved this present world too much and has gone to Thessalonica. Crescens has gone to Galatia and Titus to Dalmatia. Only Luke is still with me. Pick up Mark and bring him with you — he is a great help to me in ministry. I sent Tychicus to Ephesus.

When you come bring the coat I left with Carpus in Troas — and bring my books, especially the parchments.

Alexander the metalworker did me a great deal of harm. The Master will repay him for what he has done. Watch out for him too — he strongly opposed everything we said.

The first time I had to defend myself in court no one stood by me. Everyone abandoned me — I pray it won't be held against them. But the Master stood with me and gave me strength so that through me the message might be fully proclaimed for all the nations to hear. I was rescued from the lion's mouth. The Master will rescue me from every evil attack and bring me safely into his heavenly kingdom. To him be glory forever and ever. Amen.

Final Greetings

Give my greetings to Prisca and Aquila and to the household of Onesiphorus. Erastus stayed in Corinth and I left Trophimus in Miletus because he was sick. Do your best to get here before winter. Eubulus, Pudens, Linus, Claudia and all the brothers and sisters send their greetings.

The Master be with your spirit. Grace be with you all.
