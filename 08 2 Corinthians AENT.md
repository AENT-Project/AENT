2 Corinthians
A Friendly, Plain American English Translation
(AENT) Accessible English New Testament
This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.
A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."
This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.
Grace & Peace.
2 Corinthians Chapter 1
Opening Greeting
Paul, an apostle of Jesus the Messiah by the will of God, and brother Timothy — to the church of God in Corinth, along with all of God's people throughout Achaia. Grace and peace to you from God our Father and from our Master Jesus the Messiah.
The God of All Comfort
Praise be to the God and Father of our Master Jesus the Messiah — the Father of mercies and the God of all comfort. He comforts us in all our troubles so that we can comfort others in any kind of trouble with the same comfort we ourselves have received from God. For just as the sufferings of the Messiah overflow into our lives, so also through the Messiah our comfort overflows. If we are troubled, it is for your comfort and salvation. If we are comforted, it is for your comfort — and that comfort works powerfully in you when you endure the same sufferings we endure. Our hope for you is unshakeable, because we know that just as you share in our suffering, you also share in our comfort.
Pressure Beyond the Limit
Brothers and sisters, we don't want you to be unaware of the trouble we faced in Asia. We were crushed beyond our strength — so completely overwhelmed that we despaired of life itself. In fact, we felt as if we had been handed a death sentence. But that happened so we would stop relying on ourselves and start relying on God, who raises the dead. He rescued us from that terrible situation, and he will rescue us again. We have set our hope firmly on him — and you help us by praying for us. Then many people will give thanks to God on our behalf for the answer he gives to those many prayers.
Paul's Honesty and the Yes of God
Here is what we are proud of — our conscience confirms it. We have conducted ourselves in this world, and especially toward you, with the simplicity and sincerity that comes from God. Not by human wisdom but by the grace of God. We aren't writing anything to you beyond what you can read and understand. And I hope you will come to understand us fully — just as you have already understood us in part — because on the day of our Master Jesus, you will be our reason to boast and we will be yours.
Because I was confident of all this, I planned to visit you first so you could receive a double blessing. I planned to stop with you on my way to Macedonia, then come back to you from Macedonia, and have you send me on my way to Judea. Now when I planned this, was I being indecisive? Do I make plans the way the world does — saying yes and then no at the same time? No. As God is faithful, our word to you has never been yes and no. The Son of God, Jesus the Messiah, who was preached among you by me, Silvanus, and Timothy — he was never yes and no. In him it has always been yes. Every promise God has ever made finds its yes in him. And so through him we say our amen to the glory of God. It is God who makes us and you stand firm in the Messiah. He anointed us, sealed us, and gave us his Spirit in our hearts as a guarantee of what is still to come.
The Reason for the Delay
I call God as my witness — the reason I didn't come back to Corinth was to spare you. Not that we have any authority over your faith — we are simply fellow workers for your joy. It is by faith that you stand firm.

2 Corinthians Chapter 2
The Decision to Wait
So I made up my mind — I wasn't going to visit you again if it only meant bringing more sadness. Think about it — if I make you sad, who is left to cheer me up except the very people I've made miserable? That's exactly why I wrote what I did in my last letter. I didn't want to show up and be hurt by the very people who should be making me happy. I was completely confident in all of you, knowing that if I'm happy you're happy too. I wrote that letter while I was in deep distress, with a heavy heart and many tears. My goal wasn't to make you feel bad — but to let you know just how much I love you.
Forgive the One Who Caused Pain
Now if someone has caused pain, they haven't just hurt me — they've affected all of you to some degree, and I don't want to overstate it. The punishment handed out by the majority is enough. So instead of holding it against them, turn around and forgive them and comfort them. Otherwise that person might be completely crushed by too much sorrow. So I'm urging you to show them your love again — make it official.
The reason I wrote to you was to test you — to see if you are truly obedient in everything. If you forgive someone, I forgive them too. And whatever I have forgiven — if there was anything to forgive — I've done it for your sake in the presence of the Messiah. We do this so that Satan doesn't get the upper hand on us. We aren't ignorant of the way his mind works or the schemes he plots.
The Aroma of the Messiah
When I arrived in Troas to preach the good news of the Messiah, the Master opened a door of opportunity for me. But even then I couldn't find any peace of mind because my brother Titus wasn't there. So I said goodbye to the people there and headed to Macedonia.
But thank God! Through the Messiah he always leads us in a victory parade. Everywhere we go he uses us to spread the aroma of the knowledge of him like a sweet perfume. To God we are the sweet fragrance of the Messiah — rising up among those who are being saved and among those who are perishing. To some we smell like death leading to more death. To others we are the refreshing fragrance of life leading to more life. Who is actually qualified for a job like this?
We aren't like so many others who water down the Word of God and peddle it for profit. On the contrary — we speak with complete sincerity as people sent from God, standing in his very presence, united with the Messiah.

2 Corinthians Chapter 3
You Are Our Letter
Are we starting to sound like we're bragging again? Or do we need — like some people do — letters of recommendation to you or from you? Of course not. You yourselves are our letter, written right on our hearts, open for everyone to see and read. It's clear that you are a letter from the Messiah, produced through our ministry — written not with ink but with the Spirit of the living God, and not carved into stone tablets but into human hearts.
The New Covenant
We have this confidence before God because of the Messiah. It's not that we are qualified on our own — our qualification comes entirely from God. He is the one who made us capable of serving a new covenant — a covenant not of written rules but of the Spirit. Because the written rule brings death, but the Spirit gives life.
The Greater Glory
Think about the ministry that brought death — the one engraved in letters on stone. It arrived with such glory that the people of Israel couldn't even look at Moses' face because it was shining — even though that glory was already fading. If that ministry had glory, how much more glorious is the ministry of the Spirit? If the ministry that brought condemnation was glorious, the ministry that makes us right with God overflows with even greater glory. In fact, the glory of the past is nothing compared to the glory we have now. And if what was temporary came with glory, how much more glory belongs to what lasts forever.
The Veil Is Lifted
Because we have this hope we can speak very boldly. We aren't like Moses, who put a veil over his face so the people of Israel wouldn't see the glory fading away. But their minds were closed. Even to this day when the old covenant is read that same veil remains — it hasn't been lifted, because only in the Messiah is it taken away. Yes, even now whenever the writings of Moses are read a veil hangs over their hearts.
But whenever someone turns to the Master the veil is stripped away. And the Master I'm talking about is the Spirit — and where the Spirit of the Master is, there is true freedom. All of us with unveiled faces reflect the glory of the Master like a mirror. We are being transformed into that same image, moving from one level of glory to the next — and this comes from the Master, who is the Spirit.

2 Corinthians Chapter 4
We Don't Give Up
Because we have this ministry through God's mercy we don't give up or lose heart. We have walked away from shameful secret ways. We don't use trickery and we don't twist the Word of God. Instead we make the truth plain and present ourselves to every person's conscience in the sight of God.
If our good news seems hidden it is only hidden to those who are perishing. The god of this world has blinded the minds of those who don't believe so they cannot see the light of the good news — the glory of the Messiah, who is the perfect image of God. We aren't preaching about ourselves — we are preaching that Jesus the Messiah is Master, and that we are simply your servants for his sake. For God, who said "Let light shine out of darkness," made his light shine in our hearts to give us the light of the knowledge of his glory seen in the face of Jesus the Messiah.
Treasure in Clay Jars
But we carry this incredible treasure in clay jars — our weak human bodies — to make it clear that this overwhelming power comes from God and not from us. We are pressured on every side but not crushed. We are puzzled but not driven to despair. We are hunted down but never abandoned by God. We get knocked down but we are not destroyed. We always carry in our bodies the dying of Jesus so that his life can also be clearly seen in our bodies. While we are alive we are constantly being handed over to death for Jesus's sake so that his life may be seen in our mortal flesh. So death is at work in us — but life is at work in you.
We Believe and Therefore We Speak
We have that same spirit of faith the Scripture speaks of — "I believed, therefore I spoke." We also believe and therefore we speak. We know that the same God who raised the Master Jesus will also raise us with Jesus and bring us into his presence along with you. All of this is for your benefit — so that as grace reaches more and more people there will be a great overflow of thanksgiving to the glory of God.
Fix Your Eyes on What Lasts
So we don't give up. Even though our physical body is wearing out our inner self is being renewed every single day. Our small temporary troubles are producing for us an eternal weight of glory that goes far beyond anything we can imagine. So we don't focus on what we can see but on what we cannot see — because what we can see is temporary, but what we cannot see lasts forever.

2 Corinthians Chapter 5
Our Eternal Home
We know that if this tent we live in — our physical body — is torn down, we have a permanent home from God waiting for us. A house in heaven not made by human hands that lasts forever. Right now we groan in these bodies, deeply longing to be clothed with our heavenly home. When we are clothed with it we won't be left exposed and vulnerable. While we are in this tent we groan under the burden — not because we want to leave our bodies behind, but because we want to be clothed with our new life so that what is mortal gets swallowed up by life. God himself prepared us for this and has given us the Spirit as a guarantee of what is still to come.
So we are always confident. We know that as long as we are at home in this body we are away from the Master. We walk by faith not by what we see. And we are confident — we would actually prefer to leave this body behind and be at home with the Master. So our goal is to please him whether we are here or there. Because every one of us must stand before the judgment seat of the Messiah so that each person receives what is due for what they did while in the body — whether good or bad.
Ambassadors for the Messiah
Since we understand what it means to fear the Master we try to persuade others. God knows our hearts completely and I hope you know them too. We aren't trying to brag about ourselves again — we are giving you something to be proud of so you can answer those who brag about outward appearances instead of what is in the heart. If we seem out of our minds it is for God's sake. If we are in our right mind it is for yours.
The love of the Messiah compels us — because we are convinced that one died for all and therefore all died. He died for all so that those who live would no longer live for themselves but for him who died for them and was raised again.
So from now on we don't look at anyone from a merely human point of view. Even though we once thought of the Messiah that way we don't anymore. If anyone is in the Messiah they are a new creation. The old is gone — look, everything is new!
The Ministry of Reconciliation
All of this comes from God, who brought us back to himself through the Messiah and gave us this ministry of reconciliation. God was in the Messiah reconciling the world to himself — not counting people's sins against them. And he has committed this message of reconciliation to us.
So we are ambassadors for the Messiah. God is making his appeal through us. We are asking you on behalf of the Messiah — be reconciled to God! For God made the one who never sinned to take on our sin so that in him we might become right with God.

2 Corinthians Chapter 6
Now Is the Time
As we work together with God we urge you not to receive his grace and let it mean nothing. For he says: "At the right time I heard you, and on the day of salvation I helped you." Look — now is the right time. Today is the day of salvation.
True Servants of God
We try not to put a stumbling block in anyone's way so that no one can find fault with our ministry. Instead we show ourselves to be true servants of God in every way — by enduring suffering, hardships, and distress; through beatings, imprisonments, and riots; in hard labor, sleepless nights, and going without food. We prove ourselves by our purity, our knowledge, our patience, and our kindness — by the Holy Spirit and sincere love, by speaking the truth and by the power of God. We use the weapons of righteousness for both attack and defense.
We serve whether we are honored or despised, insulted or praised. We are treated as impostors though we are honest. As unknown though we are well known. As dying — and yet look, we are alive. As punished but not killed. As sad yet always rejoicing. As poor yet making many people rich. As having nothing yet owning everything.
Open Your Hearts to Us
We have spoken honestly to you, Corinthians. Our hearts are wide open to you. We aren't holding anything back — but you are holding back your own feelings from us. So as a fair trade — and I'm speaking to you as my own children — open your hearts wide to us too.
Don't Be Unequally Yoked
Don't team up with unbelievers. What do righteousness and wickedness have in common? What does light share with darkness? What harmony is there between the Messiah and the devil? What does a believer share with an unbeliever? What agreement can the temple of God have with idols?
We are the temple of the living God! As God said: "I will live in them and walk among them. I will be their God and they will be my people." Therefore — "Come out from among them and be separate, says God. Do not touch what is unclean and I will welcome you. I will be a Father to you and you will be my sons and daughters, says God Almighty."

2 Corinthians Chapter 7
Clean Up Your Lives
Since we have these great promises, dear friends, let's clean ourselves from everything that contaminates body and spirit. Let's pursue complete holiness because we revere God.
Paul's Joy in the Corinthians
Make room for us in your hearts. We haven't wronged anyone, corrupted anyone, or taken advantage of anyone. I'm not saying this to condemn you — as I've told you before, you are so deep in our hearts that we would die with you or live with you. I have great confidence in you and I'm very proud of you. Even with all our troubles I am full of encouragement and overflowing with joy.
When we arrived in Macedonia we had no rest at all. We were under pressure from every direction — battles on the outside and fears on the inside. But God, who comforts the discouraged, comforted us by sending Titus to us. And it wasn't just his arrival that helped — it was the encouragement you had given him. He told us how much you missed me, how sorry you were for what happened, and how deeply you care for me. That made me even happier.
Godly Sorrow vs. Worldly Sorrow
Even if my letter made you sad I don't regret it — though I did regret it for a moment when I saw it had hurt you, even if only for a little while. But now I'm glad. Not because you were hurt, but because your sadness led you to change your hearts and turn back to God. You felt the kind of sorrow God wants you to feel, so we didn't harm you at all.
Godly sorrow produces a change of heart that leads to salvation — and nobody regrets that. But the sorrow of the world only leads to death. Just look at what this godly sorrow produced in you — it made you serious, eager to clear your names, angry about sin, concerned, hungry for what is right, passionate, and determined to see justice done. In every way you proved yourselves innocent in this matter.
So even though I wrote to you it wasn't only because of the one who did the wrong or the one who was wronged. It was so that you could see for yourselves — before God — how much you truly care for us. That is why we are so encouraged.
Titus Brings Good News
On top of our own encouragement we were especially glad to see how happy Titus was — because all of you put his mind completely at ease. I had bragged about you to him and you didn't let me down. Just as everything we told you was true, our bragging to Titus about you proved to be true as well. His love for you runs even deeper now when he remembers how obedient you all were and how you welcomed him with such genuine respect. I am so glad that I can have complete confidence in you.

2 Corinthians Chapter 8
The Example of the Macedonians
Brothers and sisters, we want you to know about the grace God has shown to the churches in Macedonia. Even though they have been going through very hard times, their joy was so great that they gave generously — even out of their deep poverty. I can tell you for a fact — they gave as much as they could afford and even more than they could afford.
They did this entirely on their own, actually begging us for the privilege of sharing in this service to God's people in Jerusalem. They went far beyond what we expected. First they gave themselves to the Master, and then to us — just as God wanted. Because of this we asked Titus — who had already started this work among you — to help you finish this generous act as well.
Excel in Giving Too
You excel in everything — in faith, in speaking, in knowledge, in total dedication, and in your love for us. Now we want you to excel in giving too. I'm not ordering you to do this. I'm simply comparing your dedication with that of others to see if your love is genuine.
You know the grace of our Master Jesus the Messiah — even though he was rich he became poor for your sake, so that through his poverty you might become rich.
Here is my advice — last year you were the first ones who wanted to give and the first to start. Now finish the job. Let your results match the enthusiasm you had when you started. Give from what you actually have. If you are eager to give God accepts your gift based on what you have, not what you don't have.
Fairness and Equality
Our goal isn't to make life easy for others while making it hard for you. We want things to be fair. Right now your plenty can supply what others need, so that one day their plenty can supply what you need. That way things balance out. As the Scripture says: "The one who gathered a lot didn't have too much, and the one who gathered a little didn't have too little."
Titus and His Companions
We thank God, who put the same deep concern for you into the heart of Titus. Not only did he welcome our request to visit you — he was already so eager that he decided to come on his own. We are sending another brother with him, someone highly respected by all the churches for his work in spreading the good news. The churches also chose him to travel with us as we handle this generous gift — doing this to honor the Master and to show our willingness to help.
We are being very careful with this large sum of money. We want to do what is right and honorable — not just in the eyes of the Master but in the eyes of people too. We are also sending a third brother with them, someone we have tested many times and found to be completely dedicated. He is even more excited now because of the great confidence he has in you.
If anyone asks about Titus — he is my partner and fellow worker serving you. As for the other brothers, they are representatives of the churches and a credit to the Messiah. So show these men the proof of your love, and show all the churches why we are so proud of you.

2 Corinthians Chapter 9
Be Ready to Give
I don't really need to write to you about this gift for God's people in Jerusalem. I know how willing you are. In fact I've been bragging about you to the Macedonians — telling them that you in Achaia have been ready to give since last year. Your enthusiasm actually motivated most of them to get moving.
But I'm sending these brothers to you anyway. I don't want our boasting about you to turn out to be empty talk. I want you to be as ready as I said you would be. Otherwise if some Macedonians come with me and find you unprepared we would be embarrassed — and so would you — after all our confidence in you. So I thought it was necessary to ask these brothers to visit you ahead of time and help you finish organizing the generous gift you promised. That way it will be ready as a true willing offering and not something squeezed out of you like a tax.
Plant Generously
Remember this — the one who plants only a few seeds will get a small harvest, but the one who plants generously will have a generous harvest. Each person should give whatever they have decided in their heart to give. Don't give half-heartedly or because you feel pressured — because God loves a cheerful giver.
And God is able to shower you with every kind of grace. He can give you more than enough so that you always have everything you need and plenty left over for every good work. As the Scripture says: "He scattered his gifts freely. He gave to the poor. His righteousness lasts forever."
The Fruit of Generosity
The one who supplies seed to the farmer and bread for food will supply and multiply what you have to give. He will increase the harvest of your righteousness. You will be made rich in every way so that you can be generous on every occasion — and when we take your gifts to those who need them it will result in many people giving thanks to God.
This service you are performing does two things — it meets the needs of God's people, and it overflows in a wave of thanksgiving to God. Because of what this service proves, people will praise God for your obedience to the good news of the Messiah and for your generous sharing with them and with everyone else.
And as they pray for you they will long to see you because of the incredible grace of God resting on your lives.
Thank God for his gift that is too wonderful for words!

2 Corinthians Chapter 10
Paul's Personal Appeal
I, Paul, am making a personal appeal to you — with the gentleness and kindness of the Messiah. Some say I am timid when I'm with you in person but bold only when I'm far away. I'm asking you plainly — don't make me have to be bold when I arrive. I am fully prepared to take a stand against those who think we live by merely human standards.
We do live in this world, but we don't fight our battles the way the world does. The weapons we use are not human weapons — they are powerful through God to tear down strongholds. We tear down false arguments and every arrogant barrier raised up against the knowledge of God. We take every thought captive and make it obedient to the Messiah. And we are ready to deal with every act of disobedience once your own obedience is complete.
Don't Judge by Appearances
Look at the facts right in front of you. If anyone is confident they belong to the Messiah they should think again — we belong to the Messiah just as much as they do. Even if I brag a little too much about the authority the Master gave us I'm not ashamed of it. He gave us this authority to build you up, not tear you down.
I don't want it to seem like I'm trying to frighten you with my letters. Some people say "his letters are demanding and powerful but in person he is weak and his speaking is pathetic." Those people should realize that what we say in our letters when we are away is exactly what we will do when we arrive.
Brag in the Lord
We wouldn't dare put ourselves in the same class as those who write their own recommendation letters. They measure themselves by themselves and compare themselves with each other — they have no idea what they're doing.
We will not brag about things outside our assigned territory. We will stay within the boundaries God set for us — which includes our work among you. We aren't overstepping, as if we had never reached you. We were the first ones to come all the way to you with the good news of the Messiah.
We don't brag about work other people have done in someone else's territory. Our hope is that as your faith grows our work among you will grow as well — reaching out beyond you into new regions, preaching the good news where it hasn't been heard yet, without taking credit for what others have already done.
As the Scripture says — the one who brags should brag in the Lord. Because it isn't the person who recommends themselves who is approved — it's the person the Lord recommends.

2 Corinthians Chapter 11
Pure Devotion to the Messiah
Bear with me in a little foolishness — and actually you're already doing that. I am jealous for you with a jealousy that comes from God himself. I promised you to one husband — the Messiah — so that I could present you to him as a pure virgin. But I'm afraid that just as the serpent tricked Eve with his craftiness your minds might somehow be led astray from a simple and pure devotion to the Messiah.
If someone comes and preaches a different Jesus than the one we preached, or if you receive a different spirit than the one you received, or a different good news than the one you accepted — you seem to put up with it easily enough. But I don't consider myself inferior in any way to these so-called super-apostles. Even if I'm not a polished speaker I certainly don't lack knowledge. We have made this crystal clear to you in every possible way.
Paul Preached Free of Charge
Did I sin by humbling myself to lift you up? I preached the good news of God to you free of charge. I took support from other churches so I could serve you without cost. When I was with you and needed something I didn't burden anyone — the brothers who came from Macedonia supplied everything I needed. I have kept myself from being a burden to you in any way and I will keep on doing so. As surely as the truth of the Messiah is in me, no one in all of Achaia will take this boast away from me. Why would I do this? Because I don't love you? God knows I do.
And I will keep doing what I'm doing — to cut the ground out from under those who want an excuse to claim their work is just like ours. These people are false apostles and deceitful workers disguising themselves as apostles of the Messiah. And no wonder — Satan himself disguises himself as an angel of light. So it's no surprise if his servants disguise themselves as servants of righteousness. In the end they will get exactly what their actions deserve.
Paul's Credentials
I'll say it again — don't think of me as a fool. But if you do, at least welcome me as a fool so I can brag a little too. What I'm about to say doesn't come from the Master's leading — I'm speaking as a fool in this bragging. Since so many others are bragging about merely human things I will brag too. You are so wise that you happily put up with fools! You even put up with it when someone enslaves you, exploits you, takes advantage of you, puts on airs, or hits you in the face. I have to say — we were apparently too gentle for that.
But whatever anyone else dares to brag about — and I'm speaking like a fool now — I dare to brag about it too. Are they Hebrews? So am I. Are they Israelites? So am I. Are they descendants of Abraham? So am I. Are they servants of the Messiah? I'm talking like a madman now — but I am even more so.
A Record of Suffering
I have worked much harder, been in prison more often, been whipped more times than I can count, and faced death again and again. Five times the Jewish leaders gave me thirty-nine lashes. Three times I was beaten with rods. Once I was stoned. Three times I was shipwrecked — and once I spent a full night and day adrift at sea.
I have been constantly on the move — facing dangers from flooded rivers, dangers from robbers, dangers from my own people, and dangers from the Gentiles. Dangers in the city, dangers in the wilderness, dangers at sea, and dangers from people who claimed to be brothers but weren't. I have known hard work and exhaustion, many sleepless nights, hunger and thirst, going without food, and suffering from cold without adequate clothing.
And beyond all of that — the daily pressure of my deep concern for all the churches. Who is weak without me feeling that weakness? Who is led into sin without me burning with concern?
Weakness Is My Boast
If I have to brag I will brag about the things that show my weakness. The God and Father of our Master Jesus — who is to be praised forever — knows I am not lying. In Damascus the governor under King Aretas had the city guarded to capture me. But I was lowered in a basket through a window in the wall and slipped right out of his hands.

2 Corinthians Chapter 12
Visions and Revelations
I have to keep on with this bragging — it doesn't really accomplish anything, but bear with me as I move on to visions and revelations from the Master. I know a man in the Messiah who fourteen years ago was caught up to the third heaven. Whether he was in his body or out of it I don't know — only God knows. And I know that this man — again whether in the body or apart from it I don't know, God knows — was caught up into Paradise. He heard things so amazing they can't be put into words, things no human is permitted to repeat.
I will brag about a man like that — but I won't brag about myself except to talk about my weaknesses. Even if I wanted to brag I wouldn't be a fool for doing it because I'd be telling the truth. But I'll stop — because I don't want anyone to think more highly of me than what they actually see in me or hear from me.
The Thorn in the Flesh
To keep me from becoming conceited because of these extraordinary revelations I was given a thorn in my flesh — a messenger of Satan sent to torment me and keep me from getting too proud. Three times I begged the Master to take it away from me. But he said to me — "My grace is all you need. My power is made perfect in weakness."
So now I am glad to brag about my weaknesses so that the power of the Messiah can rest on me. That's why I actually take pleasure in weaknesses, insults, hardships, persecutions, and difficulties for the sake of the Messiah. For when I am weak — then I am truly strong.
You Should Have Defended Me
I have been acting like a fool — but you forced me into it. You are the ones who should have been defending me. Even though I am nothing I am in no way inferior to these so-called super-apostles. The signs that prove I am an apostle were performed among you with great patience — signs, wonders, and miracles. In what way were you treated worse than the other churches? Only in this — I didn't burden you for money. Please forgive me for that terrible wrong!
I Want You, Not Your Money
Now I am ready to visit you for the third time and I still won't be a burden to you. I don't want your money — I want you. Children shouldn't have to save up for their parents — parents save up for their children. I will gladly spend everything I have and spend myself completely for your souls. If I love you more does that mean you love me less?
Some might say — sure he didn't charge us but he was sneaky and got what he wanted through the men he sent. Did I take advantage of you through any of them? I urged Titus to go and sent our brother with him. Did Titus take advantage of you? Didn't we walk in the same spirit and follow the same path?
Paul's Fears for the Corinthians
Perhaps you think we have been making excuses all this time. No — we speak in the presence of God as followers of the Messiah. Everything we do, dear friends, is to build you up. But I'm afraid that when I come I might not find you the way I want you to be — and you might not find me the way you want me to be either. I'm afraid I'll find fighting, jealousy, anger, selfish ambition, slander, gossip, arrogance, and disorder. I'm afraid that when I come my God will humble me before you and I will have to mourn over many who sinned in the past and still haven't turned away from the impurity, sexual sin, and shameful behavior they've been practicing.

2 Corinthians Chapter 13
Final Warnings
This is the third time I am coming to visit you. As the Scripture says — "Every matter must be established by the testimony of two or three witnesses." I already warned those who sinned in the past and I am warning everyone else now, just as I did during my second visit. Even though I am away right now the message is the same — when I come back I will not go easy on anyone.
You want proof that the Messiah speaks through me. He is not weak when dealing with you — he is powerful among you. It's true that he was crucified in weakness, but he lives today by the power of God. In the same way we are weak in him, but by the power of God we will live with him in serving you.
Examine Yourselves
Examine yourselves to see if you are truly living in the faith. Test yourselves. Don't you realize that Jesus the Messiah lives in you? Unless of course you fail the test. And I hope you will see that we have not failed it.
We pray to God that you won't do anything wrong — not so that we look successful, but because we want you to do what is right even if it makes us look like we've failed. We can't work against the truth — only for it. We are glad to be weak if it means you are strong. Our prayer is for your complete restoration.
I am writing these things while I am away so that when I arrive I won't have to be harsh. The authority the Master gave me is for building you up — not tearing you down.
Final Greetings
Brothers and sisters — be joyful. Work toward restoration. Encourage one another. Be of one mind and live in peace. Then the God of love and peace will be with you.
Greet one another with a holy kiss. All of God's people here send their greetings.
May the grace of the Master Jesus the Messiah, the love of God, and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with all of you.
