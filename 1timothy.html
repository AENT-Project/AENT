1 Timothy

1 Timothy — (AENT) Accessible English New Testament

This version is written for everyone. We use familiar names like "Jesus" and "God" rather than the original Aramaic or Hebrew names because our goal is simple — make the Bible easy to read and understand.

A quick note: Whenever you see THE LORD in all capitals, it refers to the personal, sacred name of God. When you see Lord in normal letters, it is a title of respect, similar to calling someone "Sir" or "Master."

This text is meant as a companion to your current favorite version of the New Testament — not a replacement. Nourish your soul in the Word, every way and every chance you get.

Grace & Peace.

1 Timothy Chapter 1

Opening Greeting

Paul, an apostle of Jesus the Messiah by the command of God our Savior and of Jesus the Messiah our hope — to Timothy, my true son in the faith. Grace, mercy, and peace to you from God the Father and our Master Jesus the Messiah.

Warning Against False Teaching

When I left for Macedonia I urged you to stay in Ephesus so you could command certain people to stop teaching false doctrines. Tell them to stop getting distracted by myths and endless genealogies — these things only lead to useless debates rather than advancing God's work which is based on faith.

The goal of this command is love that comes from a pure heart, a clear conscience, and sincere faith. Some people have missed the mark and turned away to meaningless talk. They want to be teachers of the law but they don't understand what they are saying or the things they insist on so confidently.

The Purpose of the Law

We know that the law is good if one uses it properly. We also know that the law isn't made for the righteous — it is made for those who break it and rebel. For the ungodly and sinful, the unholy and profane, those who kill their fathers or mothers, murderers, the sexually immoral, those who practice homosexuality, slave traders, liars, and those who commit perjury — and for anything else that goes against healthy teaching. This is according to the glorious good news of the blessed God which was entrusted to me.

Gratitude for Mercy

I thank Jesus the Messiah our Master who has given me strength. He considered me faithful and appointed me to this ministry — even though I used to be a blasphemer, a persecutor, and a violent man. But I was shown mercy because I acted in ignorance and unbelief. The grace of our Master overflowed for me along with the faith and love that are in the Messiah Jesus.

Here is a trustworthy saying that deserves full acceptance — the Messiah Jesus came into the world to save sinners. And I am the worst of them. But for this very reason I was shown mercy — so that in me, the worst of sinners, Jesus the Messiah might demonstrate his perfect patience as an example for those who would believe in him and receive eternal life.

To the King of the ages — immortal, invisible, the only God — be honor and glory forever and ever. Amen.

Fight the Good Fight

Timothy my son — I am giving you this instruction in keeping with the prophecies once made about you. Use them to fight the good fight. Hold on to faith and a clear conscience — which some have rejected and so shipwrecked their faith. Among them are Hymenaeus and Alexander whom I have handed over to Satan so they may learn not to blaspheme.

1 Timothy Chapter 2

Pray for Everyone

First of all I urge that petitions, prayers, intercessions, and thanksgivings be made for everyone. Pray for kings and all those in positions of authority — so that we may live peaceful and quiet lives in all godliness and dignity.

This is good and pleases God our Savior, who wants all people to be saved and to come to the full knowledge of the truth. For there is only one God and there is only one Mediator between God and humanity — the man Jesus the Messiah. He gave himself as a ransom for everyone — a testimony given at just the right time. This is why I was appointed as a herald and an apostle — I am telling the truth, I am not lying — to be a teacher of the Gentiles in faith and truth.

How Men Should Pray

So I want the men everywhere to pray — lifting up holy hands without anger or arguing.

How Women Should Conduct Themselves

In the same way I want the women to dress modestly, with decency and propriety. Not with elaborate hairstyles, gold, pearls, or expensive clothes — but with good deeds, as is right for women who claim to worship God.

A woman should learn in quietness and full submission. I do not permit a woman to teach or to assume authority over a man — she is to remain quiet. For Adam was formed first, then Eve. And Adam was not the one deceived — it was the woman who was deceived and became a transgressor. But women will be preserved through childbirth if they continue in faith, love, and holiness with self-control.

1 Timothy Chapter 3

Qualifications for Overseers

Here is a trustworthy saying — if anyone aspires to be an overseer he desires a noble task. Now an overseer must be beyond reproach — the husband of one wife, clear-headed, self-controlled, respectable, hospitable, and able to teach. He must not be a drunkard or a violent man but gentle, not argumentative, and not a lover of money.

He must manage his own household well and see that his children obey him with proper respect. After all if someone doesn't know how to manage his own family how can he take care of the church of God? He should not be a new convert or he might become prideful and fall under the same judgment as the devil. He must also have a good reputation with outsiders so that he doesn't fall into disgrace and into the devil's trap.

Qualifications for Deacons

In the same way deacons must be worthy of respect — sincere, not overindulging in wine, and not greedy for dishonest gain. They must hold onto the deep truths of the faith with a clear conscience. They should be tested first and if there is nothing against them let them serve.

In the same way the women must be worthy of respect — not malicious talkers but clear-headed and trustworthy in everything.

A deacon must be the husband of one wife and must manage his children and his household well. Those who serve well gain an excellent standing and great confidence in their faith in the Messiah Jesus.

The Mystery of Godliness

I am writing these things to you hoping to come to you soon. But if I am delayed you will know how people ought to conduct themselves in the household of God — which is the church of the living God, the pillar and foundation of the truth.

Beyond all question the mystery from which true godliness springs is great —

He appeared in the flesh, Was vindicated by the Spirit, Was seen by angels, Was proclaimed among the nations, Was believed on in the world, Was taken up in glory.

1 Timothy Chapter 4

Warning Against False Teachers

The Spirit clearly says that in later times some will abandon the faith. They will follow deceptive spirits and teachings that come from demons. These teachings come through hypocritical liars whose consciences have been seared as with a hot iron.

They forbid people to marry and order them to stay away from certain foods — foods that God created to be received with thanksgiving by those who believe and know the truth. Everything God created is good and nothing is to be rejected if it is received with thanksgiving — because it is made holy by the word of God and by prayer.

Be a Good Servant of the Messiah

If you point these things out to the brothers and sisters you will be a good servant of the Messiah Jesus — nourished by the words of the faith and the sound teaching you have followed. Have nothing to do with godless myths and old wives' tales. Instead train yourself to be godly.

Physical training has some value — but godliness is valuable for everything. It holds promise for both the life we have now and the life to come. This is a trustworthy saying that deserves full acceptance. This is why we work hard and struggle — because we have put our hope in the living God who is the Savior of all people, especially of those who believe.

Set an Example

Command and teach these things. Don't let anyone look down on you because you are young — set an example for the believers in your speech, your conduct, your love, your faith, and your purity.

Until I get there focus on the public reading of Scripture, on preaching, and on teaching. Don't neglect the spiritual gift within you — the one given to you through prophecy when the elders laid their hands on you.

Give yourself completely to these things so that everyone can see your progress. Watch your life and your teaching closely. Persevere in them — because if you do you will save both yourself and those who listen to you.

1 Timothy Chapter 5

Treat Everyone Like Family

Don't speak harshly to an older man — encourage him as you would your own father. Treat younger men like brothers, older women like mothers, and younger women like sisters — always with complete purity.

Care for Widows

Show proper respect to widows who are truly alone and in need. But if a widow has children or grandchildren they should first learn to put their faith into practice by caring for their own family. This is how they repay their parents and grandparents — and it pleases God.

A woman who is truly alone puts her hope in God and spends her days and nights in prayer asking for his help. But a widow who lives only for pleasure is spiritually dead even while she is still alive. Give these instructions to the community so that no one can be blamed for neglect.

Listen closely — if someone doesn't provide for their own relatives and especially for their own immediate family they have turned their back on the faith. They are actually worse than an unbeliever.

Guidelines for the Support List

A widow should only be placed on the official support list if she is at least sixty years old and was faithful to her husband. She must be well known for good works — raising her children well, showing hospitality, serving God's people humbly, helping those in trouble, and dedicating herself to every kind of good work.

Don't put younger widows on that list. When their natural desires draw them away from the Messiah they will want to remarry — and then they feel guilty for breaking their earlier commitment. Besides that they can fall into the habit of being idle, going from house to house — not just idle but gossips and busybodies, saying things they shouldn't.

So my advice is for younger widows to remarry, have children, and manage their homes. This way they give our enemies no reason to criticize us. Sadly some have already wandered away to follow Satan. If a believing woman has widows in her family she should care for them so the church isn't burdened — and the church can focus its resources on widows who are truly alone.

Honor the Elders

Elders who lead well should be respected and honored — especially those who work hard at preaching and teaching. As Scripture says — don't muzzle an ox while it is treading out the grain, and the worker is worthy of his wages.

Don't entertain an accusation against an elder unless it is backed up by two or three witnesses. Those who are sinning should be corrected publicly so that the rest of the community takes it seriously.

Handle Everything Without Favoritism

I charge you before God and the Messiah Jesus and the chosen angels — keep these instructions without prejudice and without favoritism.

Don't be in a hurry to appoint anyone to leadership. Don't share in the sins of others. Keep yourself pure.

And a personal note — stop drinking only water. Use a little wine for the sake of your stomach and your frequent illnesses.

The sins of some people are obvious and lead straight to judgment. The sins of others don't show up until later. In the same way good works are usually obvious — and even when they aren't they can't stay hidden forever.

1 Timothy Chapter 6

Respect in the Workplace

All who work under someone's authority should show their employers full respect — so that the name of God and our teaching are not brought into disrepute. Those who have believing employers should not show them less respect just because they are brothers in the faith. Instead they should serve them even better — because those who benefit from their work are believers and are loved. Teach these things and encourage everyone to follow them.

False Teaching Leads Nowhere

If anyone teaches something different and doesn't agree with the sound words of our Master Jesus the Messiah and with the teaching that leads to true godliness — they are puffed up with pride and understand nothing. They have a morbid obsession with controversies and arguments about words. This only leads to envy, strife, malicious talk, and evil suspicions — constant friction between people whose minds are corrupt and who have lost track of the truth. These are people who think godliness is just a way to make money.

True Wealth Is Contentment

But godliness with contentment is great wealth. We brought nothing into this world and we can carry nothing out of it. So if we have food and clothing we will be content with that.

People who are determined to get rich fall into temptation and a trap. They are caught by many foolish and harmful desires that drag people down into ruin and destruction. For the love of money is a root of all kinds of evil. By chasing it some have wandered away from the faith and pierced themselves through with many sorrows.

Fight the Good Fight

But you — man of God — run from all that. Instead pursue righteousness, godliness, faith, love, endurance, and gentleness. Fight the good fight of the faith. Take hold of the eternal life to which you were called when you made your good confession before many witnesses.

In the sight of God who gives life to everything and of Jesus the Messiah who gave a good testimony before Pontius Pilate — I charge you. Keep this command without spot or blame until the appearing of our Master Jesus the Messiah.

At the right time God will bring this about — he is the blessed and only Sovereign, the King of kings and Lord of lords. He alone is immortal and lives in unapproachable light. No one has ever seen him or can see him. To him be honor and eternal power. Amen.

A Word to the Wealthy

Command those who are rich in this present world not to be arrogant or to put their hope in uncertain wealth — but to put their hope in God who richly provides us with everything for our enjoyment. Command them to do good, to be rich in good works, generous and willing to share. In this way they store up treasure for themselves as a firm foundation for the age to come — so that they may take hold of the life that is truly life.

Guard What Has Been Entrusted to You

Timothy — guard what has been entrusted to your care. Turn away from godless empty chatter and the opposing ideas of what is falsely called knowledge. Some people by claiming to have this knowledge have missed the mark concerning the faith.

Grace be with you all.
